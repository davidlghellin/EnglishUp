# Describing Photos - Describir Fotos

## Introduction - Introducción

Learning to describe photos is an important skill for English exams and everyday conversation. You will use:
- **Past Continuous** for actions happening in the photo (background)
- **Past Simple** for events that happened
- **Position words** to say where things are in the photo

Aprender a describir fotos es una habilidad importante para exámenes y conversación. Usarás:
- **Past Continuous** para acciones que ocurren en la foto (fondo)
- **Past Simple** para eventos que sucedieron
- **Palabras de posición** para decir dónde están las cosas

## Using Past Continuous - Usar el Pasado Continuo

Use Past Continuous to describe actions happening in the photo:

**Structure:** Subject + was/were + verb-ing

### Examples:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Un hombre estaba caminando | A man **was walking** |
| Los niños estaban jugando | The children **were playing** |
| Una mujer estaba leyendo | A woman **was reading** |
| El sol estaba brillando | The sun **was shining** |
| Estaba lloviendo | It **was raining** |
| La gente estaba sonriendo | People **were smiling** |

**Complete example:**
```
In the center of the photo, two children were playing football. On the left,
a woman was sitting on a bench and reading a book. In the background, some
people were walking their dogs.
```

## Using Past Simple - Usar el Pasado Simple

Use Past Simple when describing what happened or completed actions:

### Examples:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Esta foto fue tomada en la playa | This photo **was taken** at the beach |
| Fuimos allí el verano pasado | We **went** there last summer |
| Tomé esta foto en 2020 | I **took** this photo in 2020 |
| Pasamos un gran día | We **had** a great day |

## Complete Photo Description Example - Ejemplo Completo

### Example 1: Beach Photo

```
In this photo, we can see a beautiful beach. The photo was taken last summer
during my holiday.

In the foreground, there are two children. They were building a sandcastle
and they looked very happy. Next to them, a red bucket and a blue spade were
lying on the sand.

On the left, a man was swimming in the sea. In the background, we can see
some boats. The sun was shining and the sky was blue.

In the top right corner, there were some clouds. On the right, a woman was
sitting under a beach umbrella. She was reading a magazine.

It was a perfect day at the beach!
```

### Example 2: Park Photo

```
This picture shows a park on a sunny day. I took this photo last weekend.

In the center, a group of teenagers were playing basketball. They were
wearing sports clothes. One boy was jumping to throw the ball into the
basket.

In the background, we can see tall trees. The leaves were green and the
sun was shining through them.

On the left side of the photo, an old man was walking his dog. The dog
was brown and white. In the bottom right corner, two girls were sitting
on the grass. They were having a picnic and talking.

At the top of the photo, the sky was clear and blue. It looked like a
beautiful spring day.
```

### Example 3: City Street Photo

```
In this photo, we can see a busy city street. The photo was taken in the
evening, around 6pm.

In the foreground, many people were walking. Some of them were carrying
shopping bags. A woman in a red coat was talking on her phone.

In the center, there was a street musician. He was playing the guitar and
singing. A few people stopped to listen to him.

On the right, we can see several shops. Their lights were on and they
looked very bright. In the background, there were tall buildings.

In the top left corner, the sky was getting dark. Street lights were
starting to turn on. It was a typical busy evening in the city.
```

## Structure for Photo Descriptions - Estructura para Descripciones

Follow this structure:

### 1. Introduction (1-2 sentences)
- What is in the photo?
- When/where was it taken?

**Example:** *In this photo, we can see a park. The photo was taken last summer.*

### 2. Foreground (2-3 sentences)
- What's happening in front?
- Who are the main subjects?

**Example:** *In the foreground, two children were playing. They were running and laughing.*

### 3. Background (2-3 sentences)
- What can you see behind?
- What was the setting/environment?

**Example:** *In the background, there were trees and a lake. The sun was shining.*

### 4. Left and Right (2-3 sentences)
- What was on the sides?

**Example:** *On the left, a man was walking his dog. On the right, there was a bench.*

### 5. Details and Conclusion (1-2 sentences)
- Additional details
- Your impression or feeling

**Example:** *The weather looked beautiful. It seemed like a perfect day.*

## Describing Your Favourite Photo (Exam Task)

This is a common exam task: describe a photo you like and explain why.

### Task Instructions
- Describe a photo you like
- Provide information about who took it, when and where
- Explain why you like it
- Use Past Continuous (was/were + -ing)
- Use connectors: **but, because, although**
- Write 100-120 words in 30 minutes

### Structure for "My Favourite Photo"

1. **Introduction**: This is my favourite photo
2. **Description**: What's in the photo (use Past Continuous)
3. **Difficulty**: Was it easy or difficult to take? Why?
4. **Why you like it**: Your feelings about the photo
5. **Where you keep it**: What you do with the photo now

### Example: Student's Favourite Photo (Mark: 9/10)

**Teacher's comments**: Good job! Well done! Keep using your imagination and creativity!

#### Original (with corrections):
- ~~These~~ → **This** (singular)
- ~~grand~~ → **pyramid** (clarity)
- ~~there spark~~ → **there was a spark**
- ~~of movie~~ → **of a movie** (article)
- ~~makes smile~~ → **makes me smile** (object pronoun)

#### Corrected version:

```
This is my favourite photo.

We were fishing in the Nile River. In the background, we can see a pyramid
and some clouds with a spark in the sky. We were fishing a baby shark.
Suddenly, there was a spark in the sky. It was very weird.

The photo was a little easy to take because we had the camera and we were
already outside, but it was difficult because the weather was bad and there
was a storm.

I like this photo because it is funny and strange. It looks like a scene
of a movie. I have the photo on my wall at home, next to another photo
with my girlfriend. I see it every day, and it makes me smile and I
remember that moment with my friend.
```

## Sample Exam-Style Question

**Describe this photo:**

Imagine you see a photo of a family picnic in a park on a summer day.

Write a description (100-120 words) including:
- Where things and people are located (use position words)
- What people were doing (use Past Continuous)
- The weather and atmosphere
- Your general impression

**Model Answer:**

```
In this photo, we can see a family having a picnic in a park. The photo
was taken on a sunny summer day.

In the foreground, a family of four was sitting on a red blanket. The
parents were sitting on the left and their two children were on the right.
They were eating sandwiches and fruit. The mother was pouring drinks.

In the center, there was a picnic basket full of food. Next to it, a small
dog was lying down and sleeping.

In the background, we can see tall green trees. The sun was shining through
the leaves. On the right side, other families were playing games.

At the top of the photo, the sky was clear and blue. It looked like a
perfect day for a picnic. Everyone was smiling and looked very happy.
```

## Tips for Describing Photos - Consejos

1. **Start general, then specific** - Begin with what the photo shows overall, then add details

2. **Use position words** - This helps organize your description: foreground, background, left, right, corners

3. **Past Continuous for actions** - Use was/were + -ing for things happening in the photo

4. **Past Simple for facts** - Use Past Simple to say when the photo was taken or what happened

5. **Describe systematically** - Go from front to back, or from left to right, or from center outward

6. **Add atmosphere** - Describe the weather, the mood, the feeling

7. **Conclude with impression** - End with your overall feeling about the photo
