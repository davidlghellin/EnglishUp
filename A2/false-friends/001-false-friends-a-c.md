# False Friends A-C - Falsos Amigos A-C

## A

### Actually

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Actualmente | De hecho / En realidad | Actualmente = **Currently / Now / At present** | **Actually** = De hecho / En realidad |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: Actually, I'm working in Madrid. (if you mean "now")
✅ **Correct**: **Currently**, I'm working in Madrid. / I'm **now** working in Madrid.

✅ **Correct use of "actually"**:
- I thought the movie was boring, but **actually** it was quite good. (En realidad)
- She looks young, but **actually** she's 45. (De hecho)
- **Actually**, I don't like coffee. (En realidad)

**Usage:**
- **Actually** = de hecho, en realidad (to correct or clarify something)
- **Currently / Now / At present** = actualmente (at this time)

### Argument

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Argumento (de una película/libro) | Discusión / Pelea | Argumento (plot) = **Plot / Storyline** | **Argument** = Discusión / Pelea |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: The argument of the movie is very interesting. (if you mean the plot)
✅ **Correct**: The **plot** of the movie is very interesting.

✅ **Correct use of "argument"**:
- They had an **argument** about money. (Tuvieron una discusión)
- I don't want to have an **argument** with you. (No quiero discutir)
- We had a big **argument** yesterday. (Tuvimos una pelea)

**Usage:**
- **Argument** = discusión, pelea (a disagreement)
- **Plot / Storyline** = argumento (of a movie/book)

---

### Assist

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Asistir (ir a un lugar) | Ayudar | Asistir (ir a) = **Attend** | **Assist** = Ayudar |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I assisted the meeting yesterday. (if you mean you went there)
✅ **Correct**: I **attended** the meeting yesterday.

✅ **Correct use of "assist"**:
- Can you **assist** me with this? (¿Me puedes ayudar?)
- The nurse **assisted** the doctor. (La enfermera ayudó al doctor)
- I'll **assist** you with your homework. (Te ayudaré)

**Usage:**
- **Assist** = ayudar (to help)
- **Attend** = asistir a, ir a (to go to an event)

---

### Attend

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Atender (servir, cuidar) | Asistir / Ir a | Atender = **Serve / Help / Take care of** | **Attend** = Asistir / Ir a |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: The waiter attended me very well. (if you mean served you)
✅ **Correct**: The waiter **served** me very well.

✅ **Correct use of "attend"**:
- I **attend** English classes every Monday. (Asisto a)
- She **attended** the wedding. (Asistió a la boda)
- How many people **attended** the concert? (¿Cuánta gente fue al concierto?)

**Usage:**
- **Attend** = asistir a, ir a (to go to an event/class)
- **Serve** = atender (in a restaurant/shop)
- **Help / Take care of** = atender (to help someone)

## B

### Billion

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Billón (millón de millones) | Mil millones | Billón = **Trillion** | **Billion** = Mil millones |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: The world has 8 billions of people. (wrong number and grammar)
✅ **Correct**: The world has 8 **billion** people. (8,000,000,000)

**Numbers:**
- **Million** = 1,000,000 (un millón)
- **Billion** = 1,000,000,000 (mil millones)
- **Trillion** = 1,000,000,000,000 (un billón)

**Note**: In English, "billion" doesn't change in plural after a number: "3 billion" (NOT "3 billions")

## C

### Carpet

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Carpeta (folder) | Alfombra | Carpeta = **Folder** | **Carpet** = Alfombra |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I put the documents in the carpet. (if you mean folder)
✅ **Correct**: I put the documents in the **folder**.

✅ **Correct use of "carpet"**:
- We have a red **carpet** in the living room. (Tenemos una alfombra roja)
- The **carpet** is very soft. (La alfombra es muy suave)
- I need to clean the **carpet**. (Necesito limpiar la alfombra)

**Usage:**
- **Carpet** = alfombra (floor covering)
- **Folder** = carpeta (for documents)

### College

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Colegio (escuela primaria/secundaria) | Universidad / Instituto superior | Colegio = **School** | **College** = Universidad / Instituto |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: My son goes to college. He's 8 years old. (if you mean school)
✅ **Correct**: My son goes to **school**. He's 8 years old.

✅ **Correct use of "college"**:
- She's studying medicine at **college**. (Está estudiando en la universidad)
- I went to **college** in London. (Fui a la universidad)
- After high school, I want to go to **college**. (Después del instituto)

**Usage:**
- **College** = universidad, instituto superior (higher education)
- **School** = colegio, escuela (primary/secondary education)
- **High school** = instituto (secondary school, ages 14-18)

### Conductor

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Conductor (de un coche) | Director (de orquesta) / Revisor (de tren) | Conductor (coche) = **Driver** | **Conductor** = Director de orquesta / Revisor |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: The conductor of the bus was very friendly. (if you mean driver)
✅ **Correct**: The **driver** of the bus was very friendly.

✅ **Correct use of "conductor"**:
- The **conductor** raised his baton and the orchestra began. (Director de orquesta)
- The train **conductor** checked our tickets. (El revisor del tren)

**Usage:**
- **Conductor** = director de orquesta / revisor de tren
- **Driver** = conductor (of a car/bus/truck)

### Constipated

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Constipado (resfriado) | Estreñido | Constipado/Resfriado = **I have a cold** | **Constipated** = Estreñido |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I'm constipated. I have a runny nose. (very embarrassing mistake!)
✅ **Correct**: I **have a cold**. I have a runny nose.

✅ **Correct use of "constipated"**:
- He's **constipated**. He needs to eat more fiber. (Está estreñido)

**Usage:**
- **Constipated** = estreñido (digestive problem)
- **I have a cold** = estoy constipado/resfriado
- **Cold (noun)** = resfriado

**⚠️ Warning**: This is one of the most embarrassing false friends! Be very careful!

---

### Contest

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Contestar | Concurso / Competencia | Contestar = **Answer** | **Contest** = Concurso / Competencia |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: Please contest the question. (if you mean answer)
✅ **Correct**: Please **answer** the question.

✅ **Correct use of "contest"**:
- She won the singing **contest**. (Ganó el concurso de canto)
- I entered a **contest** to win a car. (Participé en un concurso)
- They organized a beauty **contest**. (Organizaron un concurso de belleza)

**Usage:**
- **Contest** = concurso, competencia (competition)
- **Answer** = contestar, responder (to reply)
