# False Friends D-L - Falsos Amigos D-L

## D

### Disgust

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Disgusto (tristeza, preocupación) | Asco / Repugnancia | Disgusto = **Upset / Annoyance / Worry** | **Disgust** = Asco / Repugnancia |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I felt disgust when I heard the bad news. (if you mean upset)
✅ **Correct**: I felt **upset** when I heard the bad news.

✅ **Correct use of "disgust"**:
- The smell filled me with **disgust**. (El olor me dio asco)
- He looked at the dirty toilet with **disgust**. (Miró con asco)
- That video is **disgusting**! (¡Ese video es asqueroso!)

**Usage:**
- **Disgust** = asco, repugnancia (strong dislike)
- **Upset** = disgustado, molesto (sad/worried)
- **Annoyance** = disgusto, molestia (irritation)

## E

### Embarrassed

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Embarazada | Avergonzado / Con vergüenza | Embarazada = **Pregnant** | **Embarrassed** = Avergonzado |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: She's embarrassed. The baby will arrive in May. (very confusing!)
✅ **Correct**: She's **pregnant**. The baby will arrive in May.

✅ **Correct use of "embarrassed"**:
- I felt so **embarrassed** when I fell. (Me sentí muy avergonzado)
- She was **embarrassed** to speak in public. (Le daba vergüenza)
- That's so **embarrassing**! (¡Qué vergüenza!)

**Usage:**
- **Embarrassed** = avergonzado, con vergüenza (feeling shame)
- **Pregnant** = embarazada (expecting a baby)

**⚠️ Warning**: This is a very important false friend to avoid confusion!

### Eventually

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Eventualmente (posiblemente) | Finalmente / Al final | Eventualmente = **Possibly / Maybe** | **Eventually** = Finalmente / Al final |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: Eventually, I will go to the party. (if you mean maybe)
✅ **Correct**: **Maybe** I will go to the party.

✅ **Correct use of "eventually"**:
- I studied hard and **eventually** I passed the exam. (Finalmente)
- He **eventually** arrived at 10pm. (Al final llegó)
- She will learn English **eventually**. (Con el tiempo / Eventualmente aprenderá)

**Usage:**
- **Eventually** = finalmente, al final (after some time)
- **Possibly / Maybe / Perhaps** = posiblemente, eventualmente (uncertainty)

## I

### Intoxicated

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Intoxicado (envenenado) | Borracho / Ebrio | Intoxicado = **Poisoned** | **Intoxicated** = Borracho |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: He ate bad food and got intoxicated. (if you mean poisoned)
✅ **Correct**: He ate bad food and got **poisoned** / got **food poisoning**.

✅ **Correct use of "intoxicated"**:
- The driver was **intoxicated**. (El conductor estaba ebrio)
- It's illegal to drive while **intoxicated**. (Conducir borracho)

**Usage:**
- **Intoxicated** = borracho, ebrio (drunk) - formal/legal term
- **Drunk** = borracho (informal)
- **Poisoned** = intoxicado, envenenado (by poison/bad food)
- **Food poisoning** = intoxicación alimentaria

## L

### Large

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Largo (long) | Grande | Largo = **Long** | **Large** = Grande |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: The street is very large. (if you mean long)
✅ **Correct**: The street is very **long**.

✅ **Correct use of "large"**:
- This shirt is too **large** for me. (Esta camisa es muy grande)
- They have a **large** house. (Tienen una casa grande)
- I'd like a **large** coffee, please. (Un café grande)

**Usage:**
- **Large** = grande (big in size)
- **Long** = largo (length, distance, time)

**Examples with both:**
- A **long** table (una mesa larga) ≠ A **large** table (una mesa grande)
- A **long** street (una calle larga) ≠ A **large** street (una calle ancha)

### Library

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Librería (bookshop) | Biblioteca | Librería = **Bookshop / Bookstore** | **Library** = Biblioteca |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I bought this book at the library. (if you mean bookshop)
✅ **Correct**: I bought this book at the **bookshop**.

✅ **Correct use of "library"**:
- I borrowed a book from the **library**. (Pedí prestado un libro de la biblioteca)
- The **library** is open until 8pm. (La biblioteca está abierta)
- Let's study at the **library**. (Estudiemos en la biblioteca)

**Usage:**
- **Library** = biblioteca (place to borrow books)
- **Bookshop / Bookstore** = librería (place to buy books)
