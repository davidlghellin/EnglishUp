# False Friends M-Z - Falsos Amigos M-Z

## M

### Misery

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Miseria (pobreza extrema) | Sufrimiento / Desdicha | Miseria (pobreza) = **Extreme poverty** | **Misery** = Sufrimiento / Desdicha |

**Examples:**

✅ **Correct use of "misery"**:
- He was in **misery** after the breakup. (Estaba sufriendo)
- The rainy weather made her feel **miserable**. (Desdichada/Triste)
- What a **miserable** day! (¡Qué día tan horrible!)

**Usage:**
- **Misery** = sufrimiento, desdicha (great suffering)
- **Miserable** = desdichado, triste, horrible
- **Extreme poverty / Destitution** = miseria (very poor condition)

## N

### Notice

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Noticia (news) | Aviso / Darse cuenta | Noticia = **News** | **Notice** = Aviso / Darse cuenta |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: Did you see the notice about the accident? (if you mean news)
✅ **Correct**: Did you see the **news** about the accident?

✅ **Correct use of "notice"**:
- Did you **notice** her new haircut? (¿Te diste cuenta de su nuevo corte?)
- I didn't **notice** you were here. (No me di cuenta)
- There's a **notice** on the board. (Hay un aviso en el tablón)
- You must give two weeks' **notice**. (Debes dar un aviso de dos semanas)

**Usage:**
- **Notice** (verb) = darse cuenta, notar
- **Notice** (noun) = aviso, anuncio
- **News** = noticia(s)

## P

### Parents

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Parientes | Padres (mamá y papá) | Parientes = **Relatives** | **Parents** = Padres |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: All my parents came to the wedding. (if you mean relatives)
✅ **Correct**: All my **relatives** came to the wedding.

✅ **Correct use of "parents"**:
- My **parents** live in Madrid. (Mis padres)
- I'm visiting my **parents** this weekend. (Voy a visitar a mis padres)
- Her **parents** are very strict. (Sus padres son muy estrictos)

**Usage:**
- **Parents** = padres (mother and father)
- **Relatives** = parientes, familiares (extended family)
- **Family** = familia

### Pretend

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Pretender (want/intend) | Fingir / Simular | Pretender = **Intend / Want / Aim to** | **Pretend** = Fingir / Simular |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I pretend to be a doctor. (if you mean you want to be)
✅ **Correct**: I **want to be** / **intend to become** a doctor.

✅ **Correct use of "pretend"**:
- She's **pretending** to be asleep. (Está fingiendo estar dormida)
- Stop **pretending**! I know you're lying. (¡Deja de fingir!)
- The children are **pretending** to be superheroes. (Están jugando a ser)

**Usage:**
- **Pretend** = fingir, simular (to act like something is true when it's not)
- **Intend / Want / Aim to** = pretender (to plan/desire)

## R

### Realize

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Realizar (do/carry out) | Darse cuenta | Realizar = **Carry out / Do / Perform** | **Realize** = Darse cuenta |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: I realized the project yesterday. (if you mean completed)
✅ **Correct**: I **completed** / **finished** the project yesterday.

✅ **Correct use of "realize"**:
- I didn't **realize** you were here! (¡No me di cuenta de que estabas aquí!)
- She **realized** she forgot her keys. (Se dio cuenta de que olvidó)
- Do you **realize** what time it is? (¿Te das cuenta de qué hora es?)

**Usage:**
- **Realize** = darse cuenta (to become aware)
- **Carry out / Do / Complete / Perform** = realizar (to execute/complete)

### Suburb

| ❌ Parece significar | ✅ Realmente significa | Español correcto | English correcto |
|:---------------------|:----------------------|:-----------------|:-----------------|
| Suburbio (slum) | Barrio residencial / Afueras | Suburbio (pobre) = **Slum / Shantytown** | **Suburb** = Barrio residencial |

**Examples:**

❌ **Wrong**: They live in a poor suburb. (confusing meaning)
✅ **Correct**: They live in the **suburbs**. (Viven en las afueras - residential area)

✅ **Correct use of "suburb"**:
- We live in the **suburbs** of London. (Vivimos en las afueras de Londres)
- The **suburbs** are quiet and family-friendly. (Los barrios residenciales)
- I commute from the **suburbs** to the city. (Viajo desde las afueras)

**Usage:**
- **Suburb** = barrio residencial, afueras (residential area outside city center)
- **Slum / Shantytown** = suburbio (poor area)
