# Connectors - Conectores

## What are Connectors? - ¿Qué son los Conectores?

Connectors (also called linking words or conjunctions) are words that join sentences, clauses, or ideas together. They help your writing and speaking flow better and show relationships between ideas.

Los conectores son palabras que unen oraciones, cláusulas o ideas. Ayudan a que tu escritura y conversación fluyan mejor y muestran relaciones entre ideas.

---

## Addition - Adición (Añadir Información)

| Español | English | Posición |
|:-------:|:-------:|:---------|
| Y | And | Entre dos elementos |
| También | Also / Too / As well | Also: medio de frase / Too: final / As well: final |
| Además | Besides / Furthermore / Moreover | Al inicio de frase |

### How to Use - Cómo Usar

**And** - Conecta dos elementos similares:
- I like coffee **and** tea.
- She is smart **and** funny.
- We went to the park **and** played football.

**Also** - Añade información (posición: antes del verbo principal, después de "to be"):
- I like coffee. I **also** like tea.
- She is smart. She is **also** funny.
- I can speak English. I can **also** speak Spanish.

**Too** - Añade información (posición: final de frase):
- I like coffee. I like tea, **too**.
- She is smart and funny, **too**.
- He plays guitar and piano, **too**.

**As well** - Añade información (posición: final de frase, más formal que "too"):
- I like tea **as well**.
- She speaks French **as well**.

**Besides / Furthermore / Moreover** - Añaden información adicional (más formales):
- I don't want to go. **Besides**, I'm tired.
- The hotel was expensive. **Furthermore**, it was dirty.
- She is intelligent. **Moreover**, she is very hardworking.

## Contrast - Contraste (Mostrar Diferencia u Oposición)

| Español | English | Uso |
|:-------:|:-------:|:----|
| Pero | But | Conecta dos ideas opuestas |
| Sin embargo | However | Más formal, al inicio de la segunda frase |
| Aunque | Although / Though / Even though | Introduce una cláusula de contraste |

### How to Use - Cómo Usar

**But** - Conecta dos ideas opuestas o contrastantes:
- I like coffee **but** I don't like tea.
- She is young **but** very experienced.
- It's expensive **but** it's good quality.
- I want to go **but** I'm too busy.

❌ **Common mistake**: Don't use "but" at the beginning of a sentence in formal writing.
- But I don't like tea. (Informal/Speaking ✓, Formal writing ✗)

**However** - Similar a "but" pero más formal (va al inicio de la segunda frase con coma):
- I like coffee. **However**, I don't like tea.
- The hotel is expensive. **However**, it's very clean.
- She studied hard. **However**, she didn't pass the exam.

**Structure**: Sentence 1. **However**, sentence 2.

**Although / Though / Even though** - Introduce una cláusula de contraste:

**Although** (más formal):
- **Although** it was raining, we went to the beach.
- I went to work **although** I was sick.
- **Although** she is rich, she is not happy.

**Though** (menos formal):
- **Though** it was late, we kept working.
- I liked the movie **though** it was long.

**Even though** (énfasis, "a pesar de que"):
- **Even though** I studied hard, I failed the exam.
- She went running **even though** it was cold.

**Structure**:
- **Although/Though/Even though** + clause 1, clause 2.
- Clause 2 + **although/though/even though** + clause 1.

❌ **Common mistake**: Don't use "but" with "although":
- ❌ Although it was raining, but we went out.
- ✅ Although it was raining, we went out.

## Reason - Razón (Explicar Por Qué)

| Español | English | Uso |
|:-------:|:-------:|:----|
| Porque | Because | Introduce la razón |
| Como / Ya que | As / Since | Introduce la razón (más formal) |

### How to Use - Cómo Usar

**Because** - Introduce la razón o causa:
- I'm happy **because** it's Friday.
- She stayed home **because** she was sick.
- We can't go out **because** it's raining.
- I like him **because** he's funny.

**Structure**:
- Result + **because** + reason
- **Because** + reason, result

**Examples**:
- I'm tired **because** I didn't sleep well.
- **Because** I didn't sleep well, I'm tired.

❌ **Common mistakes**:
- ❌ I'm tired because of I didn't sleep.
- ✅ I'm tired **because** I didn't sleep.
- ✅ I'm tired **because of** the noise. (because of + noun)

**Because vs Because of**:
- **Because** + subject + verb: I'm late **because I** missed the bus.
- **Because of** + noun: I'm late **because of** the traffic.

**As / Since** - Similar a "because" pero más formal (generalmente al inicio):
- **As** it was late, we went home.
- **Since** you're here, can you help me?
- We stayed inside **as** it was raining.

## Result - Resultado (Mostrar Consecuencia)

| Español | English | Uso |
|:-------:|:-------:|:----|
| Entonces / Así que | So | Muestra el resultado |
| Por lo tanto | Therefore / Thus | Más formal |

### How to Use - Cómo Usar

**So** - Muestra el resultado o consecuencia:
- It was raining, **so** we stayed home.
- I was tired, **so** I went to bed early.
- She studied hard, **so** she passed the exam.
- The shop was closed, **so** I couldn't buy milk.

**Structure**: Reason/Situation, **so** result.

**Therefore / Thus** - Más formales, muestran conclusión lógica:
- The weather was bad. **Therefore**, the match was cancelled.
- He didn't study. **Therefore**, he failed the exam.
- The company lost money. **Thus**, they had to close.

## Time - Tiempo (Secuencia de Eventos)

| Español | English | Uso |
|:-------:|:-------:|:----|
| Cuando | When | Momento específico |
| Mientras | While | Dos acciones al mismo tiempo |
| Antes de | Before | Acción anterior |
| Después de | After | Acción posterior |
| Entonces / Luego | Then | Siguiente paso en secuencia |
| Primero | First | Primer paso |
| Finalmente | Finally | Último paso |

### How to Use - Cómo Usar

**When** - Momento específico en que algo ocurre:
- I was sleeping **when** the phone rang.
- **When** I arrived, everyone was already there.
- Call me **when** you get home.

**While** - Dos acciones ocurriendo al mismo tiempo:
- I listen to music **while** I work.
- **While** I was cooking, he was watching TV.
- She reads **while** she eats breakfast.

**Before** - Antes de algo:
- Wash your hands **before** you eat.
- **Before** I left, I locked the door.
- I usually shower **before** I go to bed.

**After** - Después de algo:
- **After** dinner, we watched a movie.
- I'll call you **after** I finish work.
- She felt better **after** she rested.

**Then** - Siguiente acción en una secuencia:
- I woke up, **then** I had breakfast.
- First we went shopping, **then** we went to the cinema.

**First... Then... Finally** - Secuencia completa:
- **First**, I woke up. **Then**, I had breakfast. **Finally**, I went to work.

## Purpose - Propósito (Expresar Objetivo)

| Español | English | Uso |
|:-------:|:-------:|:----|
| Para | To / In order to | Expresar el objetivo |
| Para que | So that | Más formal |

### How to Use - Cómo Usar

**To / In order to** - Para expresar el propósito:
- I study English **to** improve my career.
- She exercises **to** stay healthy.
- We came early **in order to** get good seats.

**So that** - Para que (más formal):
- I study hard **so that** I can pass the exam.
- She speaks slowly **so that** everyone understands.

## Examples in Context - Ejemplos en Contexto

### Story using multiple connectors:

```
Yesterday I wanted to go to the beach because it was sunny. However, I had
a lot of work to do. First, I finished my report. Then, I cleaned my house.
Finally, I was free. Although it was already afternoon, I decided to go.

I took my towel and my book. I also took some water. When I arrived at the
beach, it was very crowded. Even though there were many people, I found a
nice spot. I swam for a while and then I read my book.

I stayed until sunset because the weather was perfect. After that, I went
home. I was tired but happy. It was a great day!
```

## Common Mistakes - Errores Comunes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct |
|:------------|:----------|
| I like tea but also coffee. | I like tea **and** coffee. / I like tea **and also** coffee. |
| Although it was late, but I stayed. | **Although** it was late, I stayed. |
| Because of I was tired, I slept. | **Because** I was tired, I slept. |
| I study English for improve my skills. | I study English **to** improve my skills. |
| She is nice. But she is shy. (formal writing) | She is nice**. However,** she is shy. |

---

**Tip**: Don't use too many connectors in one sentence. Keep your sentences clear and simple. Use connectors to show the relationship between ideas, not just to make longer sentences!
