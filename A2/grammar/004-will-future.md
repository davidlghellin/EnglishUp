# Will - Future Simple

## What is "Will"? - ¿Qué es "Will"?

"Will" is used to talk about the future. We use it for:
- Offers and promises (offering to help)
- Spontaneous decisions (deciding at the moment of speaking)
- Predictions (what we think will happen)
- Requests (asking someone to do something)

"Will" se usa para hablar del futuro. Lo usamos para:
- Ofrecimientos y promesas (ofrecer ayuda)
- Decisiones espontáneas (decidir en el momento de hablar)
- Predicciones (lo que pensamos que pasará)
- Peticiones (pedir a alguien que haga algo)

---

## Form - Formación

### Structure - Estructura:
**Subject + will + verb (base form)**

| Subject | Will | Verb | Example |
|:-------:|:----:|:----:|:--------|
| I | will | help | I **will help** you. |
| You | will | help | You **will help** me. |
| He | will | help | He **will help** us. |
| She | will | help | She **will help** them. |
| It | will | help | It **will help**. |
| We | will | help | We **will help** you. |
| They | will | help | They **will help** us. |

### Important Rules - Reglas Importantes:

✅ **Will** is the same for ALL subjects (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
✅ Use the **base form** of the verb (NOT infinitive with "to")
✅ **Never** add -s, -ed, -ing to the verb after "will"

### Short Form - Forma Corta:

| Full Form | Short Form |
|:---------:|:----------:|
| I will | I'll |
| You will | You'll |
| He will | He'll |
| She will | She'll |
| It will | It'll |
| We will | We'll |
| They will | They'll |

## Negative - Negativo

**Subject + will + not + verb (base form)**

| Subject | Will | Not | Verb | Example |
|:-------:|:----:|:---:|:----:|:--------|

## Questions - Preguntas

### Yes/No Questions - Preguntas Sí/No

**Structure - Estructura:**
**Will + subject + verb (base form)?**

| Will | Subject | Verb | Example | Answer |
|:----:|:-------:|:----:|:--------|:-------|
| Will | you | help | **Will** you **help** me? | Yes, I will. / No, I won't. |
| Will | he | come | **Will** he **come** tonight? | Yes, he will. / No, he won't. |
| Will | it | rain | **Will** it **rain** tomorrow? | Yes, it will. / No, it won't. |
| Will | they | be | **Will** they **be** there? | Yes, they will. / No, they won't. |

### Wh- Questions - Preguntas con Palabras Interrogativas

**Structure - Estructura:**
**Wh-word + will + subject + verb?**

| Question Word | Will | Subject | Verb | Example |
|:--------------|:----:|:-------:|:----:|:--------|
| What | will | you | do | **What will** you **do**? |
| Where | will | she | go | **Where will** she **go**? |
| When | will | they | arrive | **When will** they **arrive**? |
| Who | will | he | call | **Who will** he **call**? |
| How | will | we | get | **How will** we **get** there? |

### Examples - Ejemplos:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| ¿Me ayudarás? | **Will** you **help** me? |
| ¿Qué harás? | **What will** you **do**? |
| ¿Vendrá ella? | **Will** she **come**? |

| ¿Lloverá mañana? | **Will** it **rain** tomorrow? |

## Uses of "Will" - Usos de "Will"

### 1. Offers - Ofrecimientos

Offering to do something for someone:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Te ayudaré. | I**'ll help** you. |
| Abriré la puerta. | I**'ll open** the door. |
| Llevaré las bolsas. | I**'ll carry** the bags. |
| Te llevaré al aeropuerto. | I**'ll take** you to the airport. |
| Les haré un café. | I**'ll make** you a coffee. |
| Te prestaré mi coche. | I**'ll lend** you my car. |

**Common phrases:**
- I'll help you.
- I'll do it.
- I'll get it. (when the phone/door rings)
- I'll answer it.
- I'll pay for it.
- I'll call you.
- Don't worry, I'll take care of it.

### 2. Promises - Promesas

Making promises:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Te llamaré mañana. | I**'ll call** you tomorrow. |
| No lo olvidaré. | I **won't forget** it. |
| Llegaré a tiempo. | I**'ll be** on time. |
| No llegaré tarde. | I **won't be** late. |
| Te lo diré. | I**'ll tell** you. |
| Lo haré. | I**'ll do** it. |

**Common phrases:**
- I promise I'll...
- I'll never forget.
- I won't let you down.
- I won't tell anyone.
- Don't worry, I'll...

### 3. Spontaneous Decisions - Decisiones Espontáneas

Deciding at the moment of speaking (not planned):

| Situation | Decision |
|:----------|:---------|
| The phone is ringing. | I**'ll answer** it! |
| There's no milk. | I**'ll buy** some. |
| It's hot in here. | I**'ll open** the window. |
| You look tired. | I**'ll make** you a coffee. |
| The door is closed. | I**'ll get** it. |

**Examples in conversation:**

```
A: I'm hungry.
B: I'll make you a sandwich.

A: It's cold in here.
B: I'll close the window.

A: I don't have a pen.
B: I'll lend you one.

A: I can't carry all these bags.
B: Don't worry, I'll help you.
```

### 4. Predictions - Predicciones

What we think will happen in the future:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Lloverá mañana. | It **will rain** tomorrow. |
| Ella ganará el partido. | She **will win** the match. |
| Serás un buen doctor. | You **will be** a good doctor. |
| No será fácil. | It **won't be** easy. |
| Estarán felices. | They **will be** happy. |
| Todo estará bien. | Everything **will be** fine. |

**Common phrases:**
- I think it will...
- I don't think it will...
- I'm sure he will...
- I hope she will...
- Maybe they will...
- Probably it will...
- Perhaps we will...

**With "think":**
- I **think** it **will rain**.
- I **don't think** he **will come**. (NOT: I think he won't come)

### 5. Requests - Peticiones

Asking someone to do something:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| ¿Cerrarás la puerta? | **Will** you **close** the door? |
| ¿Me pasarás la sal? | **Will** you **pass** me the salt? |
| ¿Me ayudarás? | **Will** you **help** me? |
| ¿Esperarás aquí? | **Will** you **wait** here? |
| ¿Bajarás el volumen? | **Will** you **turn down** the volume? |

**Polite requests:**
- Will you please...?
- Will you help me, please?
- Would you...? (more polite)

## "Will" vs "Going to"

### Comparison - Comparación:

| Will | Going to |
|:-----|:---------|
| **Spontaneous decisions** | **Planned decisions** |
| (decided now) | (decided before) |
| Phone rings: "I**'ll answer** it!" | "I**'m going to visit** my parents this weekend." (plan) |
| **Predictions without evidence** | **Predictions with evidence** |
| "I think it **will rain** tomorrow." (opinion) | "Look at those clouds! It**'s going to rain**!" (can see clouds) |
| **Offers and promises** | **Intentions** |
| "I**'ll help** you." | "I**'m going to study** harder." |

### Examples:

**Will (spontaneous):**
- A: We don't have any bread.
- B: Oh, I**'ll buy** some! (deciding now)

**Going to (planned):**
- I**'m going to buy** some bread after work. (already planned)

**Will (no evidence):**
- I think Brazil **will win** the World Cup. (opinion)

**Going to (with evidence):**
- Look! He's going to fall! (can see he's losing balance)

## Complete Examples - Ejemplos Completos

### Conversation - Offering Help:
```
A: These bags are so heavy!
B: I'll carry them for you.
A: Thank you! That's very kind.
B: No problem. I'll take them to your car.
A: Will you help me put them in the trunk?
B: Of course! I'll do it right now.
```

### Conversation - Making Promises:
```
A: Don't forget to call me when you arrive.
B: I won't forget. I'll call you as soon as I get there.
A: Will you send me a message too?
B: Yes, I will. I promise I'll contact you.
A: OK, have a safe trip!
B: Thanks! I'll see you next week.
```

### Conversation - Spontaneous Decisions:
```
A: Oh no! I forgot to buy milk.
B: Don't worry, I'll go to the shop.
A: Really? Thank you!
B: I'll get some bread too. We're running out.
A: Great! I'll make dinner while you're out.
B: Perfect! I'll be back in 10 minutes.
```

### Making Predictions:
```
I think the weather will be nice tomorrow. The sun will shine and it
won't rain. Maybe we'll go to the beach if it's warm enough.

In the future, cars will fly and robots will do all the housework.
Life will be very different. People will live longer and work less.
It will be amazing!
```

## Common Mistakes - Errores Comunes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct |
|:------------|:----------|
| I will to help you. | I **will help** you. |
| She will helps you. | She **will help** you. |
| Will you to come? | **Will you come**? |
| I will helping you. | I **will help** you. |
| He'll comes tomorrow. | He**'ll come** tomorrow. |
| I think he won't come. | I **don't think** he **will come**. |
| I go to help you. (wrong tense) | I**'ll help** you. |

---

**Tip**: Use "will" when you decide to do something AT THE MOMENT of speaking. If you're at a restaurant and suddenly decide what to eat, say "I'll have the pasta" (NOT "I'm going to have"). Use "I'll" to sound helpful and friendly when offering to do something!
