# Past Simple - Pasado Simple

## What is the Past Simple? - ¿Qué es el Pasado Simple?

The Past Simple is used to talk about:
- Completed actions in the past (with a specific time)
- Past habits and routines
- Sequences of actions in the past

El Pasado Simple se usa para hablar de:
- Acciones completadas en el pasado (con un tiempo específico)
- Hábitos y rutinas del pasado
- Secuencias de acciones en el pasado

## Form - Formación

### Regular Verbs - Verbos Regulares

**Structure - Estructura:**
**Subject + verb + -ed**

| Infinitive | Past Simple | Example |
|:----------:|:-----------:|:--------|
| work | work**ed** | I **worked** yesterday. |
| play | play**ed** | She **played** tennis. |
| watch | watch**ed** | We **watched** a movie. |
| like | lik**ed** | They **liked** the food. |
| study | stud**ied** | He **studied** English. |
| stop | stopp**ed** | It **stopped** raining. |

### Spelling Rules for -ed - Reglas Ortográficas

| Rule | Examples |
|:-----|:---------|
| Most verbs: add **-ed** | work → work**ed**, play → play**ed**, clean → clean**ed** |
| Verbs ending in -e: add **-d** only | like → lik**ed**, live → liv**ed**, arrive → arriv**ed** |
| Verbs ending in consonant + y: change y to **-ied** | study → stud**ied**, try → tr**ied**, carry → carr**ied** |
| Verbs ending in vowel + y: add **-ed** | play → play**ed**, enjoy → enjoy**ed**, stay → stay**ed** |
| Short verbs (CVC): double last consonant + **-ed** | stop → stopp**ed**, plan → plann**ed**, drop → dropp**ed** |
| BUT: verbs ending in -w, -x, -y: don't double | snow → snow**ed**, fix → fix**ed**, play → play**ed** |

### Pronunciation of -ed - Pronunciación

| Sound | After | Examples |
|:-----:|:------|:---------|
| **/t/** | Voiceless sounds: p, k, f, s, sh, ch | work**ed** /wɜːkt/, watch**ed**, stopp**ed**, wash**ed** |
| **/d/** | Voiced sounds: b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r, and vowels | play**ed** /pleɪd/, clean**ed**, call**ed**, arriv**ed** |
| **/ɪd/** | After t or d | want**ed** /ˈwɒntɪd/, need**ed**, end**ed**, start**ed** |

## Negative - Negativo

### Structure - Estructura:
**Subject + did not (didn't) + verb (infinitive/base form)**

⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: After "didn't", use the INFINITIVE (base form) of the verb, NOT the past form!

| Subject | Didn't | Infinitive | Example |
|:-------:|:------:|:----------:|:--------|
| I | didn't | work | I **didn't work** yesterday. |
| You | didn't | go | You **didn't go** to the party. |
| He | didn't | see | He **didn't see** the movie. |
| She | didn't | come | She **didn't come** to class. |
| It | didn't | rain | It **didn't rain** yesterday. |
| We | didn't | have | We **didn't have** time. |
| They | didn't | buy | They **didn't buy** anything. |

### Examples - Ejemplos:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| No trabajé ayer. | I **didn't work** yesterday. |
| Ella no fue a Londres. | She **didn't go** to London. |
| No vimos la película. | We **didn't see** the movie. |
| No comieron nada. | They **didn't eat** anything. |
| Él no compró el coche. | He **didn't buy** the car. |
| No llovió. | It **didn't rain**. |
| No llegué tarde. | I **didn't arrive** late. |

❌ **Common mistakes**:
- ❌ I didn't went to the party.
- ✅ I didn't **go** to the party.
- ❌ She didn't saw the movie.
- ✅ She didn't **see** the movie.
- ❌ They didn't bought anything.
- ✅ They didn't **buy** anything.

## Questions - Preguntas

### Yes/No Questions - Preguntas Sí/No

**Structure - Estructura:**
**Did + subject + verb (infinitive)?**

⚠️ **IMPORTANT**: After "did", use the INFINITIVE (base form) of the verb!

| Did | Subject | Infinitive | Example | Answer |
|:---:|:-------:|:----------:|:--------|:-------|
| Did | you | work | **Did** you **work** yesterday? | Yes, I did. / No, I didn't. |
| Did | he | go | **Did** he **go** to London? | Yes, he did. / No, he didn't. |
| Did | she | see | **Did** she **see** the movie? | Yes, she did. / No, she didn't. |
| Did | they | eat | **Did** they **eat** pizza? | Yes, they did. / No, they didn't. |
| Did | it | rain | **Did** it **rain** yesterday? | Yes, it did. / No, it didn't. |

### Short Answers - Respuestas Cortas:

| Question | Affirmative | Negative |
|:---------|:-----------:|:--------:|
| Did you work? | Yes, I **did**. | No, I **didn't**. |
| Did he go? | Yes, he **did**. | No, he **didn't**. |
| Did they come? | Yes, they **did**. | No, they **didn't**. |

❌ **Don't say**: Yes, I worked. / No, I didn't worked.
✅ **Say**: Yes, I did. / No, I didn't.

## QASI Structure for Questions - Estructura QASI para Preguntas

### What is QASI? - ¿Qué es QASI?

**QASI** is a helpful acronym to remember how to form questions in Past Simple:

**Q** = **Question word** (What, Where, When, Why, How, Who, etc.)
**A** = **Auxiliary** (Did)
**S** = **Subject** (I, you, he, she, it, we, they)
**I** = **Infinitive** (base form of the verb)

### Structure - Estructura:
**Question word + DID + Subject + Infinitive?**

### Examples - Ejemplos:

| Q (Question word) | A (Auxiliary) | S (Subject) | I (Infinitive) | Complete Question |
|:-----------------:|:-------------:|:-----------:|:--------------:|:------------------|
| **What** | did | you | do | **What did** you **do** yesterday? |
| **Where** | did | she | go | **Where did** she **go**? |
| **When** | did | they | arrive | **When did** they **arrive**? |
| **Why** | did | he | leave | **Why did** he **leave**? |
| **How** | did | you | get | **How did** you **get** here? |
| **Who** | did | you | see | **Who did** you **see**? |
| **What time** | did | it | start | **What time did** it **start**? |
| **How many** | did | she | buy | **How many did** she **buy**? |

### Practice QASI - Practica QASI:

**Step by step:**

1. Choose your **Question word**: What, Where, When, Why, How, etc.
2. Add **Did** (auxiliary)
3. Add the **Subject**: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
4. Add the **Infinitive** (base form of verb): go, do, eat, see, etc.

**Examples:**

| Want to ask | Q | A | S | I | Question |
|:------------|:-:|:-:|:-:|:-:|:---------|
| About place | **Where** | did | you | go | **Where did you go**? |
| About time | **When** | did | she | arrive | **When did she arrive**? |
| About reason | **Why** | did | they | leave | **Why did they leave**? |
| About manner | **How** | did | he | do | **How did he do** it? |
| About thing | **What** | did | you | buy | **What did you buy**? |

### More Examples - Más Ejemplos:

| Español | English (QASI) |
|:-------:|:--------------|
| ¿Qué hiciste ayer? | **What did** you **do** yesterday? |
| ¿Dónde fuiste? | **Where did** you **go**? |
| ¿Cuándo llegaron? | **When did** they **arrive**? |
| ¿Por qué te fuiste? | **Why did** you **leave**? |
| ¿Cómo llegaste aquí? | **How did** you **get** here? |
| ¿A quién viste? | **Who did** you **see**? |
| ¿A qué hora empezó? | **What time did** it **start**? |
| ¿Cuánto costó? | **How much did** it **cost**? |
| ¿Cuánto tiempo pasaste allí? | **How long did** you **spend** there? |

## Exception: Questions with "Who" as Subject

When "Who" is the SUBJECT of the question, we DON'T use "did":

**Structure:** **Who + past verb** (NO did!)

| With "did" (Who = object) | Without "did" (Who = subject) |
|:--------------------------|:------------------------------|
| **Who did** you **see**? (¿A quién viste tú?) | **Who saw** you? (¿Quién te vio?) |
| **Who did** she **meet**? (¿A quién conoció ella?) | **Who met** her? (¿Quién la conoció?) |
| **Who did** they **call**? (¿A quién llamaron?) | **Who called** them? (¿Quién los llamó?) |

**More examples:**

- **Who broke** the window? (¿Quién rompió la ventana?) - Who is the subject
- **Who went** to the party? (¿Quién fue a la fiesta?) - Who is the subject
- **Who told** you? (¿Quién te lo dijo?) - Who is the subject

**Compare:**
- **Who called** you? (¿Quién te llamó?) - Who = subject, NO did
- **Who did** you **call**? (¿A quién llamaste?) - Who = object, USE did + infinitive

## Complete Examples - Ejemplos Completos

### Story in Past Simple:

```
Yesterday was a busy day. I woke up at 7am and had breakfast. Then I
went to work by bus. I arrived at 9am.

At work, I had three meetings and I wrote many emails. I didn't have
time for lunch, so I just ate a sandwich at my desk.

After work, I went to the gym. I exercised for an hour and then I went
home. I was very tired.

In the evening, I cooked dinner and watched TV. I called my mother and
we talked for 30 minutes. Finally, I went to bed at 11pm.

It was a long day, but I felt good.
```

### Conversation Using QASI:

```
A: What did you do last weekend?
B: I went to the beach. It was great!

A: Who did you go with?
B: I went with my friends.

A: How did you get there?
B: We drove. It took about two hours.

A: Where did you stay?
B: We stayed at a small hotel near the beach.

A: What did you do there?
B: We swam, played volleyball, and ate lots of seafood.

A: Did you take any photos?
B: Yes, I took hundreds! I'll show you later.

A: When did you come back?
B: We came back on Sunday evening.
```

## Common Mistakes - Errores Comunes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|:------------|:----------|:------------|
| I **don't** go to the gym this morning. | I **didn't go** to the gym this morning. | DON'T = present, DIDN'T = past! |
| I didn't went. | I didn't **go**. | Use infinitive after didn't |
| She didn't saw it. | She didn't **see** it. | Use infinitive after didn't |
| Did you worked? | Did you **work**? | Use infinitive after did |
| Where you went? | Where **did** you **go**? | Need "did" in questions (QASI) |
| What did you did? | What did you **do**? | Use infinitive after did |
| I goed to the park. | I **went** to the park. | "Go" is irregular (went) |
| She buyed a car. | She **bought** a car. | "Buy" is irregular (bought) |
| They eated pizza. | They **ate** pizza. | "Eat" is irregular (ate) |
| Yes, I worked. | Yes, I **did**. | Short answer uses "did" |

## Summary - Resumen

### Affirmative:
**Subject + past verb** (regular: -ed / irregular: special form)
- I **worked** / I **went**

### Negative:
**Subject + didn't + infinitive**
- I **didn't work** / I **didn't go**

### Questions (QASI):
**Question word + did + subject + infinitive?**
- **What did** you **do**?
- **Where did** you **go**?

### Exception: Who as subject:
**Who + past verb** (NO did)
- **Who called** you? (NOT: Who did call you?)

---

**Tip**: Practice QASI every day! Take a sentence like "I went to the park" and practice making all the question words: What did I do? Where did I go? When did I go? This will help you master Past Simple questions quickly!
