# Past Continuous - Pasado Continuo

## What is the Past Continuous? - ¿Qué es el Pasado Continuo?

The Past Continuous (also called Past Progressive) is used to talk about:
- Actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past
- Background actions in stories
- Two actions happening at the same time in the past
- Interrupted actions (with Past Simple)

El Pasado Continuo se usa para hablar de:
- Acciones que estaban en progreso en un momento específico del pasado
- Acciones de fondo en historias
- Dos acciones ocurriendo al mismo tiempo en el pasado
- Acciones interrumpidas (con Past Simple)

---

## Form - Formación

### Structure - Estructura:
**Subject + was/were + verb-ing**

| Subject | Was/Were | Verb-ing | Example |
|:-------:|:--------:|:--------:|:--------|
| I | was | working | I **was working** at 8pm. |
| You | were | working | You **were working** yesterday. |
| He | was | working | He **was working** late. |
| She | was | working | She **was working** from home. |
| It | was | raining | It **was raining** all day. |
| We | were | working | We **were working** together. |
| They | were | working | They **were working** hard. |

### Conjugation of "to be" in Past - Conjugación de "to be" en Pasado:

| Subject | Was/Were |
|:-------:|:--------:|
| I | was |
| You | were |
| He/She/It | was |
| We | were |
| They | were |

### Short Forms - Formas Cortas:

There are NO short forms for "was/were" in affirmative sentences.

❌ **Don't say**: I's working, He're working
✅ **Say**: I **was** working, He **was** working

---

## Spelling Rules for -ing - Reglas Ortográficas

(Same as Present Continuous)

| Rule | Examples |
|:-----|:---------|
| Most verbs: add **-ing** | work → work**ing**, play → play**ing**, read → read**ing** |
| Verbs ending in -e: remove -e, add **-ing** | make → mak**ing**, come → com**ing**, write → writ**ing** |
| Verbs ending in -ie: change -ie to -y, add **-ing** | lie → ly**ing**, die → dy**ing**, tie → ty**ing** |
| Short verbs (CVC): double last consonant + **-ing** | run → runn**ing**, swim → swimm**ing**, sit → sitt**ing**, stop → stopp**ing** |
| Two-syllable verbs with stress on last syllable: double | begin → beginn**ing**, prefer → preferr**ing** |

## Questions - Preguntas

### Yes/No Questions - Preguntas Sí/No

**Structure - Estructura:**
**Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?**

| Was/Were | Subject | Verb-ing | Example | Answer |
|:--------:|:-------:|:--------:|:--------|:-------|
| Was | I | working | **Was** I **working**? | Yes, you were. / No, you weren't. |
| Were | you | working | **Were** you **working**? | Yes, I was. / No, I wasn't. |
| Was | he | working | **Was** he **working**? | Yes, he was. / No, he wasn't. |
| Was | she | studying | **Was** she **studying**? | Yes, she was. / No, she wasn't. |
| Was | it | raining | **Was** it **raining**? | Yes, it was. / No, it wasn't. |
| Were | we | talking | **Were** we **talking**? | Yes, you were. / No, you weren't. |
| Were | they | playing | **Were** they **playing**? | Yes, they were. / No, they weren't. |

## When to Use - Cuándo Usar

### 1. Actions in Progress at a Specific Time in the Past

Actions that were happening at a particular moment in the past:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Estaba trabajando a las 8 de ayer. | I **was working** at 8pm yesterday. |
| Estaban cenando a las 9. | They **were having** dinner at 9. |
| Ella estaba durmiendo a medianoche. | She **was sleeping** at midnight. |
| Estábamos viendo la tele a las 7. | We **were watching** TV at 7. |

**Time expressions:**
- at 8pm yesterday
- at that time
- at that moment
- at midnight
- this time yesterday
- this time last week

**Examples:**
- What were you doing **at 10pm last night**? - I was watching TV.
- **This time yesterday**, I was sitting on the beach.
- **At that moment**, she was driving home.

---

### 2. Past Continuous + Past Simple (Interrupted Actions)

When a longer action (Past Continuous) was interrupted by a shorter action (Past Simple):

**Structure:**
- **While** + Past Continuous, Past Simple
- Past Simple + **while** + Past Continuous
- **When** + Past Simple, Past Continuous
- Past Continuous + **when** + Past Simple

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Estaba durmiendo cuando sonó el teléfono. | I **was sleeping** **when** the phone **rang**. |
| Mientras estaba cocinando, llegaron. | **While** I **was cooking**, they **arrived**. |
| Estábamos viendo la tele cuando se fue la luz. | We **were watching** TV **when** the lights **went** out. |
| Mientras ella estudiaba, yo estaba trabajando. | **While** she **was studying**, I **was working**. |

**Important:**
- Past Continuous = longer/background action (was/were + -ing)
- Past Simple = shorter/interrupting action (past verb)

**More examples:**

```
I was walking to work when I met my friend.
(longer action: walking / shorter action: met)

While she was talking on the phone, someone knocked on the door.
(background: talking / interruption: knocked)

They were playing football when it started to rain.
(background: playing / interruption: started)
```

---

### 3. Two Actions Happening at the Same Time

When two actions were happening simultaneously in the past:

**Structure:** **While** + Past Continuous, Past Continuous

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Mientras yo cocinaba, él limpiaba. | **While** I **was cooking**, he **was cleaning**. |
| Ella leía mientras yo estudiaba. | She **was reading** **while** I **was studying**. |
| Estaba lloviendo mientras caminábamos. | It **was raining while** we **were walking**. |

**Examples:**

```
While I was studying, my brother was playing video games.
(both actions happening at the same time)

She was cooking while he was watching TV.
(two simultaneous actions)

The children were playing while the adults were talking.
```

---

### 4. Background Actions in Stories

Setting the scene in narratives:

```
It was a dark night. The wind was blowing and the rain was falling.
I was walking home alone. Suddenly, I heard a strange noise...

The sun was shining. Birds were singing in the trees. Children were
playing in the park. Everything was peaceful.
```

## Past Simple vs Past Continuous

### Comparison - Comparación:

| Past Simple | Past Continuous |
|:-----------|:----------------|
| **Completed action** | **Action in progress** |
| I **worked** yesterday. (all day, finished) | I **was working** at 8pm. (at that moment) |
| **Short/interrupting action** | **Long/background action** |
| The phone **rang**. (short) | I **was sleeping**. (background) |
| **Sequence of actions** | **Simultaneous actions** |
| I **got up**, **had** breakfast, **left**. | **While** I **was cooking**, he **was reading**. |
| **Focus on the action itself** | **Focus on duration/process** |
| She **read** the book. (finished it) | She **was reading** when I called. (in the middle of it) |

### Examples:

**Past Simple:**
- I **read** a book yesterday. (completed the book)
- She **worked** from 9 to 5. (finished work)
- They **watched** a movie. (the whole movie)

**Past Continuous:**
- I **was reading** a book at 10pm. (at that moment, maybe didn't finish)
- She **was working** when I called. (in the middle of working)
- They **were watching** a movie when I arrived. (movie still playing)

**Combined:**
- I **was reading** a book **when** she **called**. (background + interruption)
- **While** she **was working**, I **arrived**. (background + event)

## Complete Examples - Ejemplos Completos

### Story Using Past Continuous and Past Simple:

```
Last night was very strange. It was 11pm and I was sitting in my living
room. I was watching a movie and eating popcorn. Suddenly, I heard a
noise outside.

I went to the window and looked out. It was raining heavily. A cat was
walking across the street. While I was watching the cat, I saw a strange
light in the sky.

I was trying to understand what it was when my phone rang. It was my
friend. She was calling to tell me about the storm. While we were talking,
the lights went out. I wasn't scared, but I was a bit worried.

After the call, I lit some candles. I was sitting in the dark when the
lights came back on. What a night!
```

### Conversation:

```
A: What were you doing at 8pm last night?
B: I was having dinner with my family.

A: What were you eating?
B: We were eating pasta. My mom was cooking while my dad was setting
   the table.

A: Were you watching TV?
B: No, we weren't. We were talking about our day.

A: What happened after dinner?
B: While I was washing the dishes, my brother was doing his homework.
   Then we all watched a movie together.
```

## Common Mistakes - Errores Comunes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|:------------|:----------|:------------|
| I was work. | I **was working**. | Need -ing form |
| She were cooking. | She **was cooking**. | Use "was" with she |
| They was playing. | They **were playing**. | Use "were" with they |
| What you were doing? | **What were** you doing? | Invert was/were |
| Were you work? | Were you **working**? | Need -ing form |
| While I watched TV, he came. | While I **was watching** TV, he came. | Use Past Continuous for background |
| I was runing. | I was **running**. | Double the 'n' |
| When she called, I watched TV. | When she called, I **was watching** TV. | Use Past Continuous for longer action |

## Summary - Resumen

### Affirmative:
**Subject + was/were + verb-ing**
- I **was working** at 8pm.
- They **were playing** football.

### Negative:
**Subject + wasn't/weren't + verb-ing**
- I **wasn't working**.
- They **weren't playing**.

### Questions:
**Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?**
- **Were** you **working**?
- **What were** you **doing**?

### Usage:
1. Actions in progress at a specific time: I **was working** at 8pm.
2. Interrupted actions: I **was sleeping** **when** the phone **rang**.
3. Simultaneous actions: **While** I **was cooking**, he **was reading**.
4. Background in stories: It **was raining** and the wind **was blowing**.

---

**Tip**: Think of Past Continuous as a "movie" (showing the action in progress) and Past Simple as a "photo" (showing a completed moment). When you combine them, the Past Continuous sets the scene (what was happening) and the Past Simple tells what happened next!
