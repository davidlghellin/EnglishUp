# Sequencing and Storytelling - Secuenciación y Narración

## What is Sequencing? - ¿Qué es la Secuenciación?

Sequencing words help us tell stories and describe events in order. They connect ideas and show when things happened. We use them with Past Simple and Past Continuous to make our stories clear and interesting.

Las palabras de secuencia nos ayudan a contar historias y describir eventos en orden. Conectan ideas y muestran cuándo pasaron las cosas. Las usamos con Past Simple y Past Continuous para hacer nuestras historias claras e interesantes.

## Time Sequence Words - Palabras de Secuencia Temporal

### Beginning a Story - Empezar una Historia

| Español | English | Example |
|:-------:|:-------:|:--------|
| El sábado pasado | Last Saturday | **Last Saturday**, I went to the beach. |
| Ayer | Yesterday | **Yesterday**, something strange happened. |
| La semana pasada | Last week | **Last week**, I met an old friend. |
| El mes pasado | Last month | **Last month**, we moved to a new house. |
| Hace dos días | Two days ago | **Two days ago**, I had an accident. |
| Un día | One day | **One day**, I was walking home when... |
| Érase una vez | Once upon a time | **Once upon a time**, there was a king. |

**Examples:**
- **Last Saturday**, I was shopping when I met my old teacher.
- **Yesterday**, while I was cooking, the doorbell rang.
- **One day**, something incredible happened.

### Continuing the Story - Continuar la Historia

| Español | English | Position | Example |
|:-------:|:-------:|:---------|:--------|
| Primero | First | Start | **First**, I woke up late. |
| Entonces / Luego | Then | Middle | I had breakfast. **Then**, I went to work. |
| Después / Después de eso | After that / Afterwards | Middle | **After that**, we went to the cinema. |
| Más tarde | Later | Middle | **Later**, I called my friend. |
| Poco después | Soon after / Shortly after | Middle | **Soon after**, it started to rain. |
| Mientras tanto | Meanwhile | Middle | **Meanwhile**, my brother was sleeping. |
| Al mismo tiempo | At the same time | Middle | **At the same time**, she was cooking. |
| Después de (+ noun/verb-ing) | After (+ noun/verb-ing) | Middle | **After dinner**, we watched TV. |

**Examples:**
- **First**, I checked my phone. **Then**, I got out of bed.
- I arrived at the station. **After that**, I bought a ticket.
- **Meanwhile**, my friends were waiting for me.

### Sudden Events - Eventos Repentinos

| Español | English | Example |
|:-------:|:-------:|:--------|
| De repente / Repentinamente | Suddenly | I was walking home when, **suddenly**, I heard a scream. |
| De pronto | All of a sudden | **All of a sudden**, the lights went out. |
| Inesperadamente | Unexpectedly | **Unexpectedly**, my old friend appeared. |
| Sin previo aviso | Without warning | **Without warning**, it started to rain. |
| En ese momento | At that moment | **At that moment**, I realized my mistake. |
| Justo entonces | Just then | **Just then**, the phone rang. |

**Important Structure:**
**Subject + was/were + verb-ing + when, suddenly, + Past Simple**

**Examples:**
- I **was sleeping** when, **suddenly**, I **heard** a loud noise.
- We **were having** dinner when, **all of a sudden**, the doorbell **rang**.
- She **was driving** home when, **unexpectedly**, her car **stopped**.
- They **were playing** football when, **suddenly**, it **started** to rain.

### Ending the Story - Terminar la Historia

| Español | English | Example |

## Story Structure - Estructura de una Historia

### 1. Setting the Scene (Past Continuous)

Start with background information using Past Continuous:

**Pattern:** It was + time/day. Subject + was/were + verb-ing.

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Era sábado. | It was Saturday. |
| Era una noche oscura. | It was a dark night. |
| Eran las 8 de la tarde. | It was 8pm. |
| Estaba lloviendo. | It was raining. |
| El sol brillaba. | The sun was shining. |

**Examples:**
```
It was last Saturday. The weather was beautiful and the sun was shining.
I was walking in the park and listening to music.
```

### 2. Main Events (Past Simple)

Tell what happened using Past Simple with sequence words:

**Pattern:** First, ... Then, ... After that, ...

**Example:**
```
First, I went to the shop. Then, I bought some bread. After that,
I walked home.
```

### 3. Interruptions (Past Continuous + Suddenly + Past Simple)

Describe when something unexpected happened:

**Pattern:** Subject + was/were + verb-ing + when, suddenly, + Past Simple

**Example:**
```
I was walking home when, suddenly, I saw my old friend. I was very
surprised!
```

### 4. Conclusion (Past Simple)

End the story:

**Pattern:** Finally, ... / In the end, ...

**Example:**
```
Finally, we said goodbye and I went home. It was a great day!
```

## Complete Story Examples - Ejemplos de Historias Completas

### Example 1: A Strange Day

```
Last Saturday, something strange happened to me. It was a beautiful
morning and the sun was shining. I was walking to the supermarket
when, suddenly, I heard a strange noise.

I stopped and looked around. At first, I couldn't see anything. Then,
I noticed a small cat sitting on a wall. The cat was meowing loudly.

I walked closer to the cat. It was wearing a collar with a name tag.
The name was "Lucky" and there was a phone number.

I called the number. A woman answered. She was very worried because
her cat was missing. I told her where I was. Ten minutes later, she
arrived.

The woman was so happy! She thanked me many times. She was crying
with joy. Finally, she took Lucky and went home.

I continued to the supermarket. I felt good about helping. It was
a strange but wonderful day!
```

### Example 2: A Rainy Adventure

```
It was last Sunday and I was at home. It was raining heavily outside
and I was reading a book. Suddenly, the doorbell rang.

I opened the door. My friend Tom was standing there. He was completely
wet! He explained that his car broke down near my house.

First, I gave him a towel. Then, we had some hot coffee. After that,
we called a mechanic. Meanwhile, the rain continued to fall.

We waited for an hour. Finally, the mechanic arrived and fixed Tom's
car. In the end, Tom thanked me and drove home.

It was an unexpected visit, but I was happy to help my friend.
```

### Example 3: An Exciting Match

```
Two weeks ago, I went to see a football match. It was Saturday evening
and the stadium was full of people. Everyone was very excited.

The match started at 7pm. My team was playing well. First, they scored
a goal in the tenth minute. Then, the other team scored too. After
that, both teams were trying hard to score.

It was the last minute of the match. My team was attacking when,
suddenly, our striker got the ball. He was running very fast. All the
fans were shouting and cheering.

Just then, he kicked the ball towards the goal. The goalkeeper jumped
but he couldn't reach it. Goal! We won 2-1!

The crowd went crazy. People were jumping and celebrating. It was an
amazing moment. Finally, the referee blew the whistle and the match
ended.

It was the most exciting match I've ever seen!
```

## Common Patterns - Patrones Comunes

### Pattern 1: Background + Sudden Event

**Structure:** Subject + was/were + verb-ing + when, suddenly, + Past Simple

| Example |
|:--------|
| I **was sleeping** when, **suddenly**, the alarm **rang**. |
| We **were having** lunch when, **all of a sudden**, the power **went** out. |
| She **was driving** when, **suddenly**, a dog **ran** into the road. |
| They **were watching** TV when, **unexpectedly**, someone **knocked** on the door. |

### Pattern 2: Sequence of Events

**Structure:** First, ... Then, ... After that, ... Finally, ...

| Example |
|:--------|
| **First**, I woke up. **Then**, I had breakfast. **After that**, I left home. **Finally**, I arrived at work. |
| **First**, we arrived at the beach. **Then**, we put on sunscreen. **After that**, we went swimming. **Finally**, we had lunch. |

### Pattern 3: Meanwhile (Two Things Happening)

**Structure:** Subject1 + Past Continuous. Meanwhile, Subject2 + Past Continuous.

| Example |
|:--------|
| I **was cooking** dinner. **Meanwhile**, my husband **was setting** the table. |
| The children **were playing** outside. **Meanwhile**, their parents **were preparing** the party. |

## Common Mistakes - Errores Comunes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|:------------|:----------|:------------|
| I was walk when suddenly I saw her. | I **was walking** when suddenly I saw her. | Need -ing form |
| Suddenly, I was seeing my friend. | Suddenly, I **saw** my friend. | Use Past Simple after "suddenly" |
| First I went, then I was buying. | First I **went**, then I **bought**. | Use Past Simple for sequence |
| I walked when suddenly it rained. | I **was walking** when suddenly it **rained**. | Use Past Continuous for background |
| It was rain. | It **was raining**. | Need -ing for weather in progress |
| Last Saturday I go to the park. | Last Saturday I **went** to the park. | Use Past Simple with past time |

## Summary - Resumen

### To tell a story, use:

**1. Time expression to start:**
- Last Saturday, Yesterday, Two days ago, Once upon a time

**2. Past Continuous for background/setting:**
- It was raining. I was walking home.

**3. Sequence words for order:**
- First, Then, After that, Finally

**4. "Suddenly" for unexpected events:**
- I was sleeping when, suddenly, the phone rang.

**5. Past Simple for main events:**
- I opened the door. A man was standing there.

**6. Conclusion:**
- Finally, everything was OK. / In the end, I went home.

---

**Tip**: When telling a story, think of it like a movie. Past Continuous describes the scene (what was happening in the background), and Past Simple describes the action (what happened). Use "suddenly" to add excitement and surprise to your stories!
