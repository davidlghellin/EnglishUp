# Going to - Future Plans

## What is "Going to"? - ¿Qué es "Going to"?

"Going to" is used to talk about the future. We use it for:
- Plans and intentions (things you decided before)
- Predictions based on evidence (what we can see will happen)

"Going to" se usa para hablar del futuro. Lo usamos para:
- Planes e intenciones (cosas que decidiste antes)
- Predicciones basadas en evidencia (lo que vemos que va a pasar)

---

## Form - Formación

### Structure - Estructura:
**Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb (base form)**

| Subject | To Be | Going to | Verb | Example |
|:-------:|:-----:|:--------:|:----:|:--------|
| I | am | going to | visit | I **am going to visit** Paris. |
| You | are | going to | travel | You **are going to travel** to Spain. |
| He | is | going to | stay | He **is going to stay** at a hotel. |
| She | is | going to | fly | She **is going to fly** to London. |
| It | is | going to | rain | It **is going to rain**. |
| We | are | going to | book | We **are going to book** a tour. |
| They | are | going to | visit | They **are going to visit** Rome. |

### Short Forms - Formas Cortas:

| Full Form | Short Form |
|:---------:|:----------:|
| I am going to | I**'m going to** |
| You are going to | You**'re going to** |
| He is going to | He**'s going to** |
| She is going to | She**'s going to** |
| It is going to | It**'s going to** |
| We are going to | We**'re going to** |
| They are going to | They**'re going to** |

### Pronunciation Note:
In spoken English, "going to" often sounds like **"gonna"** (informal):
- I'm **gonna** visit Paris. (spoken)
- I'm **going to** visit Paris. (written)

⚠️ **Important**: Only use "gonna" in very informal speech. Always write "going to"!

## When to Use "Going to" - Cuándo Usar

### 1. Plans and Intentions - Planes e Intenciones

Things you decided to do BEFORE the moment of speaking:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| Voy a visitar París el mes que viene. | I**'m going to visit** Paris next month. |
| Vamos a reservar un hotel mañana. | We**'re going to book** a hotel tomorrow. |
| Ella va a estudiar inglés. | She**'s going to study** English. |
| Van a comprar billetes hoy. | They**'re going to buy** tickets today. |

**Examples:**
- I**'m going to travel** to Spain this summer. (I already decided)
- We**'re going to stay** at a five-star hotel. (We planned it)
- She**'s going to visit** her family next week. (Her plan)

---

### 2. Predictions Based on Evidence - Predicciones con Evidencia

When you can SEE something is about to happen:

| Español | English |
|:-------:|:-------:|
| ¡Mira esas nubes! Va a llover. | Look at those clouds! It**'s going to rain**. |
| Cuidado, vas a caer. | Be careful, you**'re going to fall**! |
| El avión va a despegar. | The plane **is going to take off**. |

**Examples:**
- Look at the sky! It**'s going to rain**. (I can see dark clouds)
- Be careful! You**'re going to drop** that! (I see you're not holding it well)
- The bus **is going to leave**. (It's already moving)

## Travel Agency Scenario - Escenario de Agencia de Viajes

### Example 1: Planning a Trip to Paris

**Travel Agent:** Good morning! How can I help you?

**Customer:** Hi! I**'m going to visit** Paris next month and I need to book a trip.

**Travel Agent:** Wonderful! **How long are you going to stay**?

**Customer:** I**'m going to stay** for one week, from July 15th to July 22nd.

**Travel Agent:** Great! **Where are you going to stay**? **Are you going to book** a hotel?

**Customer:** Yes, I**'m going to stay** at a hotel in the city center. Can you recommend one?

**Travel Agent:** Of course! **What are you going to visit** while you're there?

**Customer:** I**'m going to visit** the Eiffel Tower, the Louvre Museum, and Notre-Dame. I**'m also going to take** a boat tour on the Seine River.

**Travel Agent:** Excellent! **How are you going to travel** to Paris?

**Customer:** I**'m going to fly** from Madrid. Can you book the flights too?

**Travel Agent:** Absolutely! So let me summarize: You**'re going to fly** to Paris on July 15th, you**'re going to stay** at a hotel for one week, and you**'re going to visit** the main attractions. **Are you going to need** a tour guide?

**Customer:** No, I**'m going to explore** on my own, but I**'m going to book** some tours online.

**Travel Agent:** Perfect! I**'m going to prepare** a complete itinerary for you.

## Will vs Going to - Diferencia

| Going to | Will |
|:---------|:-----|
| **Planned decisions** | **Spontaneous decisions** |
| (decided before) | (decided now) |
| I'm **going to** visit Paris next month. (planned) | Phone rings: "I**'ll** answer it!" (spontaneous) |
| **Predictions with evidence** | **Predictions without evidence** |
| (you can see it will happen) | (opinion, belief) |
| Look at those clouds! It**'s going to** rain! (see clouds) | I think it **will** rain tomorrow. (opinion) |

### Examples:

**Going to (planned):**
- I**'m going to** study English next year. (I already decided and enrolled)
- We**'re going to** visit my parents this weekend. (We planned it)

**Will (spontaneous):**
- A: We don't have any milk. B: I**'ll** buy some! (deciding now)
- A: Can you help me? B: Sure, I**'ll** help you. (offering now)

**Going to (evidence):**
- Be careful! You**'re going to** fall! (I can see you're losing balance)
- Look! The bus **is going to** leave! (It's starting to move)

**Will (no evidence):**
- I think she **will** like this gift. (my opinion)
- Maybe they **will** come to the party. (uncertain)

## Common Mistakes - Errores Comunes

| ❌ Incorrect | ✅ Correct | Explanation |
|:------------|:----------|:------------|
| I going to visit Paris. | I**'m going to** visit Paris. | Need "am/is/are" |
| She is going visit Paris. | She is going **to** visit Paris. | Need "to" |
| They are going to visits Paris. | They are going to **visit** Paris. | Use base form (infinitive) |
| Are you going visit Paris? | Are you going **to** visit Paris? | Need "to" |
| I'm going to Paris. | I'm going to **go to** Paris. (if you mean travel there) | "Going to" needs a verb |
| I will to visit Paris. | I **will** visit Paris. / I**'m going to** visit Paris. | Don't mix "will" and "to" |

**Note**: "I'm going to Paris" is correct if you mean you're traveling there now (present continuous for fixed future arrangements), but "I'm going to go to Paris" is the "going to" future form.

## Summary - Resumen

### Structure:
**Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb (base form)**

### Uses:
1. **Plans and intentions** (decided before)
   - I'm going to visit Paris next month.

2. **Predictions with evidence** (can see it will happen)
   - Look! It's going to rain!

### Questions:
**Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb?**
- Are you going to travel?
- What are you going to do?

### Negative:
**Subject + am/is/are + not + going to + verb**
- I'm not going to go.
- She isn't going to travel.

---

**Tip**: When planning a trip, use "going to" for all your plans and arrangements. It shows you've already thought about it and made decisions. Practice by writing your own travel itinerary or holiday plans using "going to" for everything!
