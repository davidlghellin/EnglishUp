# Present Perfect Simple

## Connecting the Past to the Present

The Present Perfect connects past actions or experiences to the present moment.

---

## Structure

### Affirmative

```
Subject + HAVE/HAS + Past Participle (V3)
```

| Subject | Have/Has | Past Participle | Example |
|---------|----------|-----------------|---------|
| I | have | worked | I **have worked** here for 5 years |
| You | have | seen | You **have seen** this movie |
| We | have | finished | We **have finished** the project |
| They | have | been | They **have been** to Paris |
| He | has | lived | He **has lived** here since 2020 |
| She | has | eaten | She **has eaten** sushi |
| It | has | changed | It **has changed** a lot |

### Contractions (very common!)

| Full form | Contraction |
|-----------|-------------|
| I have | I**'ve** |
| You have | You**'ve** |
| We have | We**'ve** |
| They have | They**'ve** |
| He has | He**'s** |
| She has | She**'s** |
| It has | It**'s** |

**Examples:**
- I**'ve** finished my homework
- She**'s** been to London
- We**'ve** seen that movie

---

### Negative

```
Subject + HAVE/HAS + NOT + Past Participle
```

| Full form | Contraction | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| have not | **haven't** | I **haven't** seen him |
| has not | **hasn't** | She **hasn't** arrived yet |

**Examples:**
- I **haven't finished** yet
- He **hasn't called** me
- They **haven't been** to Spain

---

### Questions

```
HAVE/HAS + Subject + Past Participle?
```

| Question | Short Answer |
|----------|--------------|
| **Have** you **seen** this film? | Yes, I have / No, I haven't |
| **Has** she **finished**? | Yes, she has / No, she hasn't |
| **Have** they **arrived**? | Yes, they have / No, they haven't |

**Wh- Questions:**
- **Where** have you been?
- **What** has she done?
- **How long** have you lived here?
- **Why** has he left?

## When to Use Present Perfect

### 1. Life Experiences (Experiencias)

Actions that happened at an unspecified time in your life.

**Key words:** ever, never, before

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **have visited** Paris | He visitado París |
| She **has never eaten** sushi | Ella nunca ha comido sushi |
| **Have** you **ever been** to Japan? | ¿Has estado alguna vez en Japón? |
| I**'ve** **seen** this movie before | He visto esta película antes |

> **Note:** We don't say WHEN (no specific time). If we say when, use Past Simple.

---

### 2. Recent Actions with Results Now (Acciones recientes)

Actions that happened recently and have a result or relevance now.

**Key words:** just, already, yet

| Word | Position | Use | Example |
|------|----------|-----|---------|
| **just** | after have/has | "hace poco" | I**'ve just** finished (Acabo de terminar) |
| **already** | after have/has | "ya" (affirmative) | She**'s already** left (Ya se ha ido) |
| **yet** | end of sentence | "todavía/ya" (negative/question) | I **haven't** finished **yet** |

**Examples:**
- I**'ve just eaten**, so I'm not hungry (Acabo de comer)
- She**'s already done** her homework (Ya ha hecho los deberes)
- **Have** you finished **yet**? (¿Ya has terminado?)
- I **haven't** called him **yet** (Todavía no le he llamado)

---

### 3. Unfinished Time Periods (Períodos no terminados)

Actions in a time period that is NOT finished (today, this week, this year).

**Key words:** today, this week/month/year, recently, lately

| Example | Explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| I**'ve had** three coffees **today** | Today is not finished |
| She**'s worked** a lot **this week** | This week is not finished |
| We**'ve traveled** a lot **this year** | This year is not finished |
| I**'ve seen** him **recently** | Recent past, connected to now |

---

### 4. Duration: FOR and SINCE (Duración)

Actions that started in the past and continue to the present.

#### FOR + period of time (duration)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I**'ve lived** here **for** 5 years | Vivo aquí desde hace 5 años |
| She**'s worked** here **for** 2 months | Trabaja aquí desde hace 2 meses |
| We**'ve been** married **for** 10 years | Llevamos 10 años casados |

**FOR + period:** for 2 hours, for 3 days, for a week, for 6 months, for years, for a long time

#### SINCE + point in time (starting point)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I**'ve lived** here **since** 2019 | Vivo aquí desde 2019 |
| She**'s worked** here **since** January | Trabaja aquí desde enero |
| We**'ve known** each other **since** school | Nos conocemos desde el colegio |

**SINCE + point:** since Monday, since January, since 2020, since I was a child, since last week

#### FOR vs SINCE

| FOR (duration) | SINCE (starting point) |
|----------------|------------------------|
| for 3 years | since 2021 |
| for 2 weeks | since Monday |
| for a month | since January |
| for ages | since I was young |
| for a long time | since last summer |

## Present Perfect vs Past Simple

This is the most important difference to understand!

### Past Simple = Finished time
### Present Perfect = Connected to now

| Past Simple | Present Perfect |
|-------------|-----------------|
| I **saw** that movie **last week** | I**'ve seen** that movie (sometime in my life) |
| She **went** to Paris **in 2019** | She**'s been** to Paris (at some point) |
| I **ate** pizza **yesterday** | I**'ve eaten** pizza **today** |
| He **worked** here **for 5 years** (but not now) | He**'s worked** here **for 5 years** (still works here) |

### Key Difference

| Use Past Simple when: | Use Present Perfect when: |
|----------------------|---------------------------|
| You say WHEN (yesterday, last week, in 2020) | You DON'T say when |
| The time period is FINISHED | The time period is NOT finished |
| The action is COMPLETELY in the past | The action CONNECTS to now |

### Examples

❌ I **have seen** him **yesterday** → ✅ I **saw** him **yesterday**
❌ I **saw** him (no time) → ✅ I**'ve seen** him (= at some point)

❌ I **have been** to Rome **in 2019** → ✅ I **went** to Rome **in 2019**
❌ I **went** to Rome (experience) → ✅ I**'ve been** to Rome

## The Conversation Pattern: Present Perfect → Past Simple

### This is KEY for real conversations!

When we talk about experiences, we follow a natural pattern:

```
1. PRESENT PERFECT → Open the topic (general question)
2. PAST SIMPLE     → Get the details (when, where, how, who with)
```

### How It Works

| Step | Tense | Why? |
|------|-------|------|
| 1. Ask about experience | **Present Perfect** | We don't know WHEN, we just ask IF |
| 2. Answer yes/no | **Present Perfect** | Still general, no time specified |
| 3. Ask for details | **Past Simple** | Now we want specifics: when, where, how |
| 4. Give details | **Past Simple** | Talking about a specific past moment |

### Example Conversations

**Conversation 1: Travel**

| Speaker | Sentence | Tense | Why? |
|---------|----------|-------|------|
| A: | **Have** you ever **been** to Japan? | Present Perfect | General experience question |
| B: | Yes, I **have**! | Present Perfect | General answer |
| A: | When **did** you **go**? | Past Simple | Asking for specific detail |
| B: | I **went** last summer. | Past Simple | Specific time |
| A: | What **did** you **do** there? | Past Simple | More details |
| B: | I **visited** Tokyo and **ate** a lot of sushi. | Past Simple | Specific actions |

**Conversation 2: Food**

| Speaker | Sentence | Tense | Why? |
|---------|----------|-------|------|
| A: | **Have** you ever **tried** Korean food? | Present Perfect | Experience? |
| B: | No, I **haven't**. | Present Perfect | No experience |
| A: | You should try it! | — | Recommendation |

> **Note**: If the answer is "No, I haven't", we stay in Present Perfect. There are no details to ask about!

**Conversation 3: Work**

| Speaker | Sentence | Tense | Why? |
|---------|----------|-------|------|
| A: | **Have** you ever **worked** remotely? | Present Perfect | Experience? |
| B: | Yes, I **have**. | Present Perfect | General |
| A: | Where **did** you **work** from? | Past Simple | Detail |
| B: | I **worked** from home for 2 years during COVID. | Past Simple | Specific period |
| A: | **Did** you **like** it? | Past Simple | Detail about that time |
| B: | Yes, I **loved** it! | Past Simple | Specific opinion |

**Conversation 4: Learning**

| Speaker | Sentence | Tense | Why? |
|---------|----------|-------|------|
| A: | **Have** you ever **studied** another language? | Present Perfect | Experience? |
| B: | Yes, I**'ve studied** French. | Present Perfect | General |
| A: | How long **did** you **study** it? | Past Simple | Detail |
| B: | I **studied** it for 3 years in school. | Past Simple | Specific period |
| A: | **Did** you **enjoy** it? | Past Simple | Detail |
| B: | Not really, I **found** it difficult. | Past Simple | Specific opinion |

### The Rule

```
Present Perfect = "Have you ever...?" (¿Alguna vez has...?)
         ↓
      YES → Switch to Past Simple for details
      NO  → Stay in Present Perfect or change topic
```

### Common Mistake

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Why? |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Have you ever been to Paris? When **have** you **gone**? | Have you ever been to Paris? When **did** you **go**? | Details need Past Simple |
| Have you tried sushi? Where **have** you **eaten** it? | Have you tried sushi? Where **did** you **eat** it? | Specific detail = Past Simple |

### Practice: Build Conversations

Start with these Present Perfect questions, then write 2-3 Past Simple follow-up questions:

1. **Have you ever been** to the US?
   - When did you go? / Where did you stay? / What did you visit?

2. **Have you ever cooked** something special?
   - What did you cook? / Who did you cook for? / Did they like it?

3. **Have you ever met** a famous person?
   - Who did you meet? / Where did you meet them? / What did they say?

4. Write your own:
   - Have you ever _______? → _______ → _______

|------------|---------|

| just | I've **just** arrived |
| already | She's **already** finished |
| yet | Have you finished **yet**? / I haven't finished **yet** |
| recently | I've seen him **recently** |
| lately | She's been busy **lately** |
| today | I've had 3 meetings **today** |
| this week/month/year | We've traveled a lot **this year** |
| for + duration | I've lived here **for** 5 years |
| since + point | I've worked here **since** 2020 |
| so far | **So far**, we've sold 100 tickets |

### With Past Simple (NOT Present Perfect!)

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| yesterday | I saw him **yesterday** |
| last week/month/year | She called **last week** |
| ago | I met him 2 years **ago** |
| in + year | They moved **in 2015** |
| when | **When** did you arrive? |
| specific date | I started **on Monday** |

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Using Past Simple with "ever/never"
❌ Did you **ever** go to Japan? → ✅ **Have** you **ever been** to Japan?
❌ I **never saw** this movie → ✅ I**'ve never seen** this movie

### 2. Using Present Perfect with specific past time
❌ I **have seen** him **yesterday** → ✅ I **saw** him **yesterday**
❌ She **has visited** Paris **in 2019** → ✅ She **visited** Paris **in 2019**

### 3. Confusing FOR and SINCE
❌ I've lived here **since** 5 years → ✅ I've lived here **for** 5 years
❌ I've known her **for** 2019 → ✅ I've known her **since** 2019

### 4. Using "gone" vs "been"
- **been** = went and came back (fue y volvió)
- **gone** = went and is still there (fue y no ha vuelto)

❌ She's **gone** to Paris (she's still in Paris)
✅ She's **been** to Paris (she visited and came back)

### 5. Wrong participle
❌ I have **went** → ✅ I have **gone**
❌ She has **ate** → ✅ She has **eaten**
❌ They have **wrote** → ✅ They have **written**

---

## BEEN vs GONE

| BEEN | GONE |
|------|------|
| Went and came back | Went and is still there |
| = visited | = left |

**Examples:**
- She**'s been** to London (fue y volvió - she's here now)
- She**'s gone** to London (se ha ido - she's in London now)

- Where have you **been**? (¿Dónde has estado?)
- Where has John **gone**? (¿Adónde ha ido John? - he's not here)
