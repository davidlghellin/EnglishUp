# Defining Relative Clauses

## Connecting Ideas with WHO, WHICH, THAT, WHERE, WHEN, WHOSE

Relative clauses give us extra information about a noun. They help us define or identify which person, thing, place, or time we are talking about.

---

## What are Relative Clauses?

A relative clause is a part of a sentence that gives more information about a noun.

**Example:**
- The man **who works here** is my friend.
- The book **that I bought** is interesting.
- The restaurant **where we ate** was expensive.

The words **who, which, that, where, when, whose** connect the relative clause to the main sentence.

---

## Relative Pronouns

| Pronoun | Use for | Example |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **WHO** | People | The woman **who** lives next door... |
| **WHICH** | Things/Animals | The car **which** is parked outside... |
| **THAT** | People or Things | The man **that** called you... / The book **that** I read... |
| **WHERE** | Places | The hotel **where** we stayed... |
| **WHEN** | Times | The day **when** we met... |
| **WHOSE** | Possession | The girl **whose** father is a doctor... |

---

## WHO - For People

Use **WHO** when you talk about people.

### Structure
```
NOUN (person) + WHO + VERB...
```

### Examples

| Sentence | Spanish |
|----------|---------|
| The man **who works** here is my brother | El hombre que trabaja aquí es mi hermano |
| I know a girl **who speaks** five languages | Conozco a una chica que habla cinco idiomas |
| The teacher **who teaches** us English is from London | El profesor que nos enseña inglés es de Londres |
| People **who live** in cities are usually busy | La gente que vive en ciudades suele estar ocupada |
| The woman **who called** you is my mother | La mujer que te llamó es mi madre |

### More Examples
- Do you know anyone **who can** help me?
- I have a friend **who is** a doctor.
- The person **who wrote** this book is famous.
- She's the girl **who won** the competition.

---

## WHICH - For Things and Animals

Use **WHICH** when you talk about things or animals.

### Structure
```
NOUN (thing/animal) + WHICH + VERB...
```

### Examples

| Sentence | Spanish |
|----------|---------|
| The car **which is** parked outside is mine | El coche que está aparcado fuera es mío |
| I bought a book **which has** 500 pages | Compré un libro que tiene 500 páginas |
| The movie **which won** the Oscar was amazing | La película que ganó el Oscar fue increíble |
| The dog **which lives** next door barks a lot | El perro que vive al lado ladra mucho |
| The computer **which I bought** is very fast | El ordenador que compré es muy rápido |

### More Examples
- This is the house **which was** built in 1900.
- I found the keys **which I lost** yesterday.
- The train **which leaves** at 9am is the fastest.
- She has a cat **which sleeps** all day.

---

## THAT - For People OR Things

**THAT** can replace both WHO and WHICH. It's very common in spoken English.

### Examples

| With WHO | With THAT |
|----------|-----------|
| The man **who** called... | The man **that** called... |
| The girl **who** lives... | The girl **that** lives... |

| With WHICH | With THAT |
|------------|-----------|
| The book **which** I read... | The book **that** I read... |
| The car **which** is... | The car **that** is... |

### When to Use THAT

- **THAT** is more common in spoken English
- **THAT** is more informal
- **WHO/WHICH** are more common in written English and formal contexts

**Examples:**
- The woman **that** works here is nice. (= who)
- The film **that** we watched was boring. (= which)
- Is this the phone **that** you lost? (= which)
- He's the guy **that** helped me. (= who)

---

## WHERE - For Places

Use **WHERE** when you talk about places.

### Structure
```
NOUN (place) + WHERE + SUBJECT + VERB...
```

### Examples

| Sentence | Spanish |
|----------|---------|
| This is the restaurant **where we ate** | Este es el restaurante donde comimos |
| The hotel **where we stayed** was expensive | El hotel donde nos quedamos era caro |
| I know a place **where you can** buy cheap clothes | Conozco un sitio donde puedes comprar ropa barata |
| The town **where I was born** is very small | El pueblo donde nací es muy pequeño |
| That's the shop **where I work** | Esa es la tienda donde trabajo |

### More Examples
- This is the park **where** we met.
- The country **where** she lives is very cold.
- Is this the street **where** you live?
- The office **where** I work is downtown.

> **Note:** You can also use "in which" instead of "where" (more formal):
> - The hotel **in which** we stayed... = The hotel **where** we stayed...

---

## WHEN - For Times

Use **WHEN** when you talk about times.

### Structure
```
NOUN (time) + WHEN + SUBJECT + VERB...
```

### Examples

| Sentence | Spanish |
|----------|---------|
| I remember the day **when we met** | Recuerdo el día que nos conocimos |
| Summer is the time **when** I feel happiest | El verano es la época cuando me siento más feliz |
| 1990 was the year **when** I was born | 1990 fue el año en que nací |
| Monday is the day **when** I have English class | El lunes es el día que tengo clase de inglés |

### More Examples
- Do you remember the moment **when** you knew?
- There are times **when** I feel lonely.
- The 1960s was a time **when** music changed.
- Friday is the day **when** we go out.

---

## WHOSE - For Possession

Use **WHOSE** to show possession (de quién).

### Structure
```
NOUN + WHOSE + NOUN + VERB...
```

### Examples

| Sentence | Spanish |
|----------|---------|
| The girl **whose father** is a doctor... | La chica cuyo padre es médico... |
| I know a man **whose wife** works at Google | Conozco a un hombre cuya esposa trabaja en Google |
| The student **whose homework** was the best... | El estudiante cuyo trabajo fue el mejor... |
| That's the woman **whose car** was stolen | Esa es la mujer a la que le robaron el coche |

### More Examples
- The boy **whose** bike is broken needs help.
- I met a writer **whose** books are famous.
- She's the person **whose** opinion I trust.
- The family **whose** house burned down needs help.

> **Note:** WHOSE can be used for people AND things:
> - The company **whose** products are popular... (cuyo)

---

## Subject vs Object Relative Clauses

### Subject Relative Clause
The relative pronoun IS the subject of the verb.

```
The man WHO WORKS here... (who = subject of "works")
The book WHICH IS on the table... (which = subject of "is")
```

### Object Relative Clause
The relative pronoun IS NOT the subject (there's another subject).

```
The man WHO I MET... (I = subject, who = object)
The book WHICH I BOUGHT... (I = subject, which = object)
```

### Can We Omit the Relative Pronoun?

**YES** - In OBJECT relative clauses (informal):
- The man (who/that) **I met** was nice.
- The book (which/that) **I bought** is good.
- The film (which/that) **we watched** was boring.

**NO** - In SUBJECT relative clauses:
- The man **who works** here... (cannot omit)
- The book **which is** on the table... (cannot omit)

---

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Using WHICH for people
❌ The man **which** works here → ✅ The man **who/that** works here

### 2. Using WHO for things
❌ The book **who** I read → ✅ The book **which/that** I read

### 3. Adding extra pronouns
❌ The man who **he** works here → ✅ The man who works here
❌ The book which I bought **it** → ✅ The book which I bought

### 4. Wrong word order
❌ The man works here who → ✅ The man **who works here**
❌ The restaurant we ate where → ✅ The restaurant **where we ate**

### 5. Using WHERE for people/things
❌ The person **where** called → ✅ The person **who** called
❌ The thing **where** I need → ✅ The thing **which/that** I need

1. The woman _______ lives next door is a nurse.
2. The film _______ we watched last night was boring.
3. I have a friend _______ speaks Japanese.
4. The pizza _______ I ordered was delicious.
5. People _______ smoke should stop.
6. The car _______ is parked outside is mine.
7. Is this the book _______ you wanted?
8. The teacher _______ teaches us is from Scotland.

2. which/that
3. who/that
4. which/that
5. who/that
6. which/that

8. who/that
</details>

Complete with where, when, or whose:

### Exercise 3: Complete the sentences

Join the two sentences using a relative pronoun:

1. I know a man. He speaks 6 languages.
   → I know a man _______________________

2. This is the book. I bought it yesterday.
   → This is the book _______________________

3. That's the restaurant. We ate there last week.
   → That's the restaurant _______________________

4. She's the girl. Her mother is a famous actress.
   → She's the girl _______________________

5. I remember the summer. I met my wife then.
   → I remember the summer _______________________

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. I know a man **who speaks** 6 languages.
2. This is the book **which/that I bought** yesterday.
3. That's the restaurant **where we ate** last week.
4. She's the girl **whose mother** is a famous actress.
5. I remember the summer **when I met** my wife.
</details>

### Exercise 4: Correct the mistakes

1. The man which called you is my uncle.
2. The book who I'm reading is very interesting.
3. This is the house where I live in it.
4. The woman whose she works here is nice.
5. I know a place where you can to eat.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. The man **who/that** called you is my uncle.
2. The book **which/that** I'm reading is very interesting.
3. This is the house where I live. (remove "in it")
4. The woman **who** works here is nice. (remove "whose she")
5. I know a place where you can eat. (remove "to")
</details>
