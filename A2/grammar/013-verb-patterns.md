# Verb Patterns: Like, Love, Hate, Enjoy + ING

## Verbs Followed by -ING (Gerund)

---

## The Rule

Some verbs are followed by **verb + ING** (gerund), NOT the infinitive.

```
LIKE / LOVE / HATE / ENJOY + VERB-ING
```

| Correct ✅ | Incorrect ❌ |
|-----------|-------------|
| I like **swimming** | ~~I like swim~~ |
| She loves **cooking** | ~~She loves cook~~ |
| He hates **waiting** | ~~He hates wait~~ |
| We enjoy **traveling** | ~~We enjoy travel~~ |

---

## Common Verbs + ING

### LIKE + ING

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **like reading** books | Me gusta leer libros |
| She **likes going** to the gym | A ella le gusta ir al gimnasio |
| They **like watching** movies | Les gusta ver películas |
| Do you **like cooking**? | ¿Te gusta cocinar? |
| I **don't like waiting** | No me gusta esperar |

### LOVE + ING

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **love traveling** | Me encanta viajar |
| She **loves dancing** | A ella le encanta bailar |
| We **love eating** out | Nos encanta comer fuera |
| He **loves playing** football | Le encanta jugar al fútbol |

### HATE + ING

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **hate getting** up early | Odio levantarme temprano |
| She **hates doing** homework | Ella odia hacer los deberes |
| They **hate waiting** in queues | Odian esperar en colas |
| He **hates cleaning** | Él odia limpiar |

### ENJOY + ING

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **enjoy walking** in the park | Disfruto caminando en el parque |
| She **enjoys reading** | Ella disfruta leyendo |
| We **enjoy spending** time together | Disfrutamos pasando tiempo juntos |
| Do you **enjoy cooking**? | ¿Disfrutas cocinando? |

## More Verbs That Take -ING

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| **finish** | I **finished working** at 6pm |
| **stop** | She **stopped smoking** last year |
| **avoid** | He **avoids eating** sugar |
| **mind** | Do you **mind waiting**? |
| **consider** | I'm **considering moving** to Madrid |
| **keep** | He **keeps talking** |
| **practice** | I **practice speaking** English every day |
| **suggest** | She **suggested going** to the beach |
| **miss** | I **miss living** in Spain |
| **imagine** | Can you **imagine living** there? |

---

## Questions and Negatives

### Questions

```
DO/DOES + subject + LIKE + verb-ING?
```

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Do** you **like cooking**? | Yes, I do. / No, I don't. |
| **Does** she **like swimming**? | Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't. |
| What do you **like doing** at weekends? | I like going to the cinema. |

### Negatives

```
Subject + DON'T/DOESN'T + LIKE + verb-ING
```

| Negative |
|----------|
| I **don't like getting** up early |
| She **doesn't like cleaning** |
| They **don't like waiting** |

---

## LIKE + ING vs LIKE + TO + infinitive

In British English, both can be used with similar meaning:

| -ING (more common) | TO + infinitive |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| I like **swimming** | I like **to swim** |
| She likes **reading** | She likes **to read** |

> **Note**: With ENJOY, HATE, LOVE → only use -ING
> - I enjoy swimming ✅
> - ~~I enjoy to swim~~ ❌

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Explanation |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| I like **go** to the gym | I like **going** to the gym | Like + -ING |
| She enjoys **swim** | She enjoys **swimming** | Enjoy + -ING |
| He hates **wait** | He hates **waiting** | Hate + -ING |
| I love **travel** | I love **traveling** | Love + -ING |
| Do you mind **help** me? | Do you mind **helping** me? | Mind + -ING |
| I finished **work** | I finished **working** | Finish + -ING |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Complete with the correct form

1. I like _______ (read) books.
2. She enjoys _______ (cook) Italian food.
3. They hate _______ (wait) in queues.
4. He loves _______ (play) video games.
5. Do you mind _______ (help) me?
6. I finished _______ (do) my homework.
7. She stopped _______ (smoke) last year.
8. We enjoy _______ (spend) time together.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. reading
2. cooking
3. waiting
4. playing
5. helping
6. doing
7. smoking
8. spending
</details>
