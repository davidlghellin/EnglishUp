# Used To - Past Habits

## Talking About Past Habits and States

---

## What is "Used To"?

We use **used to** to talk about things that were true in the past but are NOT true now.

```
USED TO + base verb = past habit/state (not anymore)
```

---

## Form

### Affirmative

```
Subject + USED TO + verb (base form)
```

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **used to** work with Arch Linux | Yo trabajaba con / solía trabajar con Arch Linux |
| She **used to** live in Madrid | Ella vivía en Madrid |
| We **used to** play football every weekend | Solíamos jugar al fútbol cada fin de semana |
| He **used to** smoke | Él fumaba (pero ya no) |

### Negative

```
Subject + DIDN'T USE TO + verb (base form)
```

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **didn't use to** like coffee | No me gustaba el café (antes) |
| She **didn't use to** work here | Ella no trabajaba aquí |
| We **didn't use to** have smartphones | No teníamos smartphones |

> **Note**: In negatives, it's "use to" (no D), not "used to"

### Questions

```
DID + subject + USE TO + verb?
```

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| **Did** you **use to** play sports? | Yes, I did. / No, I didn't. |
| **Did** she **use to** work there? | Yes, she did. |
| What **did** you **use to** do at weekends? | I used to go hiking. |

---

## When to Use "Used To"

### 1. Past Habits (repeated actions)

Things you did regularly in the past but don't do anymore:

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **used to** work with Arch Linux | I worked with it before, but not now |
| She **used to** run every morning | Regular past action, stopped now |
| We **used to** meet every Friday | We don't meet every Friday anymore |

### 2. Past States (situations)

Situations that were true before but changed:

| Example | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| I **used to** live in Barcelona | I lived there, but I moved |
| He **used to** be shy | He was shy before, not anymore |
| This **used to** be a school | It was a school, now it's something else |

---

## Used To vs Past Simple

| Used To | Past Simple |
|---------|-------------|
| Emphasizes the habit/state is OVER | Just describes past action |
| I **used to** smoke | I **smoked** a cigarette yesterday |
| (habit that ended) | (single past action) |

### Examples

| Used To (habit ended) | Past Simple (past action) |
|-----------------------|---------------------------|
| I **used to** work in Madrid | I **worked** late yesterday |
| She **used to** play tennis | She **played** tennis last week |
| We **used to** have a dog | We **had** a meeting at 3pm |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Wrong | Correct | Explanation |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| I ~~use to~~ work there | I **used to** work there | Affirmative needs "used to" |
| I ~~used to worked~~ | I **used to work** | Base verb after "used to" |
| I didn't ~~used to~~ | I didn't **use to** | Negative: "use to" (no D) |
| Did you ~~used to~~? | Did you **use to**? | Question: "use to" (no D) |
| I ~~am used to work~~ | I **used to work** | Don't confuse with "be used to" |

---

## "Used To" vs "Be Used To"

These are DIFFERENT structures:

| Structure | Meaning | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **used to** + verb | past habit (ended) | I **used to** live in Spain |
| **be used to** + noun/-ing | be accustomed to | I **am used to** living in Spain |

### Examples

| Used to (past habit) | Be used to (accustomed) |
|----------------------|-------------------------|
| I **used to** work nights | I **am used to** working nights |
| (I worked nights before) | (I'm accustomed to it now) |
| She **used to** drive on the left | She **is used to** driving on the left |
| (She did it in the past) | (She's comfortable with it) |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Complete with "used to" or "didn't use to"

1. I _______ (live) in a small town when I was a child.
2. She _______ (like) vegetables, but now she loves them.
3. We _______ (have) a garden, but we moved to an apartment.
4. He _______ (be) very fit, but he stopped exercising.
5. They _______ (travel) much before they retired.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. used to live
2. didn't use to like
3. used to have
4. used to be
5. didn't use to travel
</details>

### Exercise 2: Make questions

1. you / play / video games? → _______________________
2. she / work / here? → _______________________
3. they / live / in this neighborhood? → _______________________
4. your parents / travel / a lot? → _______________________

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Did you use to play video games?
2. Did she use to work here?
3. Did they use to live in this neighborhood?
4. Did your parents use to travel a lot?
</details>

## Tech/Work Examples

| Example | Context |
|---------|---------|
| I **used to** work with Arch Linux, but now I use Mac | Changed technology |
| We **used to** use SVN, now we use Git | Changed tools |
| The company **used to** have an office, now it's remote | Changed work style |
| I **used to** write everything in Java | Changed programming language |
| We **used to** deploy manually | Changed processes |

---

**Tip**: Use "used to" when you want to emphasize that something was a habit or state in the past that has now changed. It's perfect for talking about your previous jobs, technologies you worked with before, or how things were different in the past.
