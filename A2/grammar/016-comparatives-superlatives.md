# Comparatives & Superlatives

---

## How to form them

### Short adjectives (1 syllable): + -er / + -est

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative | Example |
|-----------|------------|-------------|---------|
| cheap | cheap**er** | the cheap**est** | Online shopping is **cheaper** than physical stores. It is **the cheapest** option. |
| fast | fast**er** | the fast**est** | Delivery is **faster** now. Amazon has **the fastest** shipping. |
| big | bi**gger** | the bi**ggest** | This store is **bigger**. It is **the biggest** in town. |
| hot | ho**tter** | the ho**ttest** | Today is **hotter** than yesterday. July is **the hottest** month. |

> **Spelling rules for short adjectives:**
> - Ends in -e → just add -r/-st: larg**er**, larg**est**
> - Ends in consonant + vowel + consonant → double the last letter: big → bi**gg**er, hot → ho**tt**er
> - Ends in -y → change to -ier/-iest: easy → eas**ier**, the eas**iest**

### Long adjectives (2+ syllables): more / the most

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative | Example |
|-----------|------------|-------------|---------|
| expensive | **more** expensive | **the most** expensive | Physical stores are **more expensive**. Gucci is **the most expensive**. |
| convenient | **more** convenient | **the most** convenient | Online shopping is **more convenient**. It is **the most convenient** way to buy. |
| practical | **more** practical | **the most** practical | It is **more practical** to shop online. |
| comfortable | **more** comfortable | **the most** comfortable | Shopping from home is **more comfortable**. |

### Irregular (hay que memorizarlos)

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|------------|-------------|
| good | **better** | **the best** |
| bad | **worse** | **the worst** |
| far | **further** / farther | **the furthest** / farthest |
| much / many | **more** | **the most** |
| little | **less** | **the least** |

## Comparative structures

| Structure | Example | Spanish |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| A is **___-er than** B | Online shopping is **cheaper than** physical stores | más ... que |
| A is **more ___ than** B | It is **more convenient than** going to a shop | más ... que |
| A is **less ___ than** B | Physical stores are **less practical than** online shops | menos ... que |
| A is **as ___ as** B | Online shopping is **as popular as** physical stores | tan ... como |
| A is **not as ___ as** B | Physical stores are **not as cheap as** online shops | no tan ... como |

---

## Superlative structures

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| A is **the ___-est** (+ noun) | Amazon is **the biggest** online store |
| A is **the most ___** (+ noun) | It is **the most convenient** option |
| A is **the least ___** (+ noun) | It is **the least expensive** shop |

## Comparatives & Superlatives with ADVERBS

> Recuerda: los adjetivos describen sustantivos, los adverbios describen verbos.
> - She is **quick** (adjective → describes "she")
> - She runs **quickly** (adverb → describes "runs")

### Short adverbs (1 syllable): same as adjectives → + -er / + -est

| Adverb | Comparative | Superlative | Example |
|--------|------------|-------------|---------|
| fast | fast**er** | the fast**est** | He runs **faster** than me. She runs **the fastest** in the class. |
| hard | hard**er** | the hard**est** | You need to work **harder**. She works **the hardest**. |
| late | lat**er** | the lat**est** | He arrived **later** than usual. |
| early | earli**er** | the earli**est** | I woke up **earlier** today. |

### Long adverbs (-ly ending): more / the most

| Adverb | Comparative | Superlative | Example |
|--------|------------|-------------|---------|
| quickly | **more** quickly | **the most** quickly | She types **more quickly** than me. |
| easily | **more** easily | **the most** easily | You can find it **more easily** online. |
| carefully | **more** carefully | **the most** carefully | He drives **more carefully** than his brother. |
| comfortably | **more** comfortably | **the most** comfortably | You can shop **more comfortably** from home. |

### Irregular adverbs

| Adverb | Comparative | Superlative |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| well | **better** | **the best** |
| badly | **worse** | **the worst** |
| far | **further** / farther | **the furthest** / farthest |
| much | **more** | **the most** |
| little | **less** | **the least** |

### Adjective vs Adverb — side by side

| Adjective (+ noun / after be) | Adverb (+ verb) |
|-------------------------------|-----------------|
| She is **faster** than me | She runs **faster** than me |
| He is a **better** player | He plays **better** than me |
| This method is **easier** | This method works **more easily** |
| Online shopping is **cheaper** | You can buy things **more cheaply** online |
| He is **more careful** | He drives **more carefully** |

> **Key:** "fast", "hard", "early", "late" tienen la misma forma como adjetivo y adverbio.
> Los adverbios que terminan en **-ly** siempre usan **more/the most** para comparar.

## Common mistakes

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Why? |
|----------|-----------|------|
| more **cheaper** | **cheaper** | Short adjective → -er, NOT more + -er |
| more **better** | **better** | Irregular, never "more better" |
| **expensiver** | **more expensive** | Long adjective → more, NOT -er |
| the **most cheapest** | the **cheapest** | Never mix "most" + "-est" |
| cheaper **that** | cheaper **than** | Always "than" for comparisons |
| It is **the more** convenient | It is **the most** convenient | Superlative = "the most", NOT "the more" |
