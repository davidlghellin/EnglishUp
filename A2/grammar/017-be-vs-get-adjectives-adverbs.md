# "be" vs "get" + Adjectives vs Adverbs

---

## "be" vs "get" (estado vs cambio de estado)

### "be" → describe a STATE (cómo es/está algo)

| English | Spanish | Explanation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| I **am** tired | Estoy cansado | State right now |
| The shop **is** closed | La tienda está cerrada | State right now |
| Online shopping **is** convenient | Las compras online son cómodas | Permanent quality |
| It **is** cheaper | Es más barato | State/quality |

### "get" → describe a CHANGE OF STATE (volverse / ponerse / hacerse)

| English | Spanish | Explanation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| I **got** tired | Me cansé | I was not tired → now I am |
| The shop **got** closed | La tienda cerró / fue cerrada | It was open → now it is closed |
| It **is getting** cheaper | Se está volviendo más barato | Change in progress |
| She **got** angry | Se enfadó | She was calm → now she is angry |
| It **gets** dark early in winter | Oscurece pronto en invierno | Change that happens |

### "feel" → describe a SUBJECTIVE PERCEPTION (cómo te sientes / te parece)

| English | Spanish | Explanation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| I **feel** tired | Me siento cansado | How I perceive myself |
| She **feels** happy | Se siente feliz | Her personal sensation |
| I **feel** cold | Tengo frío / siento frío | Subjective sensation |
| It **feels** wrong | Se siente mal / parece incorrecto | How something seems to you |
| The water **feels** warm | El agua se siente templada | How it seems when you touch it |

### Key difference: be vs feel vs get

```
"be"   = ESTADO (objective)    → I am tired     (estoy cansado — es un hecho)
"feel" = PERCEPCIÓN (subjective) → I feel tired   (me siento cansado — es mi sensación)
"get"  = CAMBIO                → I got tired    (me cansé — antes no lo estaba)
```

> **"feel" vs "be":** Son muy parecidos. "feel" es más subjetivo y personal. "be" es más objetivo y directo.
> - "I **am** happy" = Soy/estoy feliz (hecho)
> - "I **feel** happy" = Me siento feliz (percepción personal)
> - A veces son intercambiables, pero "feel" enfatiza que es tu sensación interna.

### be vs feel vs get — comparison table

| be (state) | feel (perception) | get (change) |
|------------|-------------------|--------------|
| I **am** nervous | I **feel** nervous | I **got** nervous (me puse nervioso) |
| She **is** sad | She **feels** sad | She **got** sad (se puso triste) |
| He **is** comfortable | He **feels** comfortable | He **got** comfortable (se puso cómodo) |
| It **is** hot | It **feels** hot | It **got** hot (se puso caliente) |
| We **are** better | We **feel** better | We **got** better (mejoramos) |

### When to use each one

| Situation | Use | Example |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| Objective fact | **be** | The room **is** cold (thermometer says 10°C) |
| Your personal sensation | **feel** | The room **feels** cold (to me it seems cold) |
| Something changed | **get** | The room **got** cold (it was warm before) |
| Doctor asks how you are | **feel** | I **feel** sick / I **feel** dizzy |
| Describing someone's personality | **be** | She **is** kind (es amable — siempre) |
| Emotions in a moment | **feel** or **be** | I **feel** angry / I **am** angry (both OK) |

### Other verbs that work like "feel" (+ adjective, NOT adverb)

| Verb | Example | Spanish |
|------|---------|---------|
| **look** | She **looks** tired | Parece cansada (by appearance) |
| **sound** | That **sounds** interesting | Suena interesante |
| **smell** | The food **smells** delicious | La comida huele deliciosa |
| **taste** | This coffee **tastes** bitter | Este café sabe amargo |
| **seem** | He **seems** happy | Parece feliz |

> These are **linking verbs** — they connect the subject to an adjective (like "be").
> - She looks **beautiful** ✅ (NOT ~~beautifully~~)
> - It smells **good** ✅ (NOT ~~well~~)
> - It sounds **great** ✅ (NOT ~~greatly~~)

### More examples

| State (be) | Change (get) |
|------------|-------------|
| I **am** lost | I **got** lost (me perdí) |
| She **is** married | She **got** married (se casó) |
| He **is** bored | He **got** bored (se aburrió) |
| The food **is** cold | The food **got** cold (se enfrió) |
| They **are** ready | They **got** ready (se prepararon) |
| It **is** worse | It **got** worse (empeoró) |
| We **are** used to it | We **got** used to it (nos acostumbramos) |

### Common mistake

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ |
|----------|-----------|
| I **am** lost yesterday | I **got** lost yesterday (cambio → get) |
| The weather **is getting** cold right now | ✅ (change in progress) |
| The weather **is** cold | ✅ (state right now) |

## Adjectives vs Adverbs

### Adjective → describes a NOUN (sustantivo)

| English | Spanish | What it describes |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| a **fast** car | un coche rápido | the car (noun) |
| an **easy** exercise | un ejercicio fácil | the exercise (noun) |
| a **cheap** product | un producto barato | the product (noun) |
| **online** shopping is **practical** | las compras online son prácticas | shopping (noun) |

### Adverb → describes a VERB (acción) or an adjective

| English | Spanish | What it describes |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| He drives **fast** | Conduce rápido | how he drives (verb) |
| You can return it **easily** | Puedes devolverlo fácilmente | how you return it (verb) |
| It works **correctly** | Funciona correctamente | how it works (verb) |
| It is **usually** cheaper | Suele ser más barato | modifies "cheaper" (adjective) |

### How to form adverbs

| Adjective | Adverb | Rule |
|-----------|--------|------|
| easy | eas**ily** | -y → -ily |
| correct | correct**ly** | + ly |
| cheap | cheap**ly** | + ly |
| comfortable | comfortab**ly** | -e → -ly |
| quick | quick**ly** | + ly |
| significant | significant**ly** | + ly |
| practical | practical**ly** | + ly |

### Irregular adverbs (no siguen la regla)

| Adjective | Adverb | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| good | **well** | She speaks English **well** (NOT ~~goodly~~) |
| fast | **fast** | He runs **fast** (same form) |
| hard | **hard** | She works **hard** (NOT ~~hardly~~ = apenas) |
| late | **late** | He arrived **late** (NOT ~~lately~~ = últimamente) |

### Key rule: after a verb → ADVERB

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Why? |
|----------|-----------|------|
| They fit **correct** | They fit **correctly** | "fit" is a verb → needs adverb |
| She speaks English **good** | She speaks English **well** | "speaks" is a verb → needs adverb |
| He drives **slow** | He drives **slowly** | "drives" is a verb → needs adverb |
| It works **perfect** | It works **perfectly** | "works" is a verb → needs adverb |

### Key rule: after "be" → ADJECTIVE

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Why? |
|----------|-----------|------|
| It is **cheaply** | It is **cheap** | after "is" (be) → adjective |
| She is **beautifully** | She is **beautiful** | after "is" (be) → adjective |
| They are **easily** | They are **easy** | after "are" (be) → adjective |

### Quick trick

```
VERB + ___?  → ADVERB    → She runs QUICKLY
BE + ___?    → ADJECTIVE → She is QUICK
```
