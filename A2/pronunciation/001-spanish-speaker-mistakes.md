# Pronunciation for Spanish Speakers

## Common Mistakes and How to Fix Them

---

## 1. The "E" Before S + Consonant

### The Problem

In Spanish, words never start with "s" + consonant. They always have "e" before:
- España, escuela, estudiar, especial

So Spanish speakers often say:
- ❌ "e-Spain" instead of "Spain"
- ❌ "e-school" instead of "school"
- ❌ "e-study" instead of "study"

### The Solution

**Technique 1: Start with a HISS**

1. Make a snake sound: "ssssss"
2. While hissing, add the rest of the word
3. "ssss...pain" → "Spain"

**Practice:**
```
sssss → sssspain → Spain
sssss → sssschool → school
sssss → sssstudy → study
```

**Technique 2: Connect to the previous word**

Instead of saying "I went to e-Spain", connect the words:
- "I went to Spain" → "I wentto**S**pain"
- "I go to school" → "I goto**S**chool"

The previous word "absorbs" the S sound.

**Technique 3: Whisper first**

1. Whisper the word (without voice): "Spain" 🤫
2. Notice there's NO "e" sound when you whisper
3. Now say it normally, keeping the same start

### Words to Practice

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Practice |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| e-Spain | Spain | /speɪn/ |
| e-speak | speak | /spiːk/ |
| e-special | special | /ˈspeʃəl/ |
| e-study | study | /ˈstʌdi/ |
| e-student | student | /ˈstjuːdənt/ |
| e-school | school | /skuːl/ |
| e-start | start | /stɑːt/ |
| e-stop | stop | /stɒp/ |
| e-small | small | /smɔːl/ |
| e-smile | smile | /smaɪl/ |
| e-snow | snow | /snəʊ/ |
| e-sleep | sleep | /sliːp/ |

### Daily Practice Sentences

Say these 5 times each, focusing on the S:

1. **S**pain is a beautiful country.
2. I **s**tudy English at **s**chool.
3. **S**top! Don't **s**tart yet.
4. She **s**peaks **S**panish and English.
5. It's **s**nowing! Let's **s**tay inside.

## 2. The "H" Sound

### The Problem

In Spanish, "H" is silent (hola, hora, hacer).
In English, "H" is pronounced (hello, hour is an exception!).

Spanish speakers often:
- ❌ Say "ello" instead of "hello"
- ❌ Say "appy" instead of "happy"

### The Solution

**Technique: Breathe out like fogging a mirror**

1. Pretend you're fogging up a mirror or glasses: "hhhhhh"
2. That breath is the English "H"
3. Add the rest of the word: "hhhhh...ello" → "hello"

### Words to Practice

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ | Practice |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| ello | hello | /həˈləʊ/ |
| appy | happy | /ˈhæpi/ |
| ouse | house | /haʊs/ |
| otel | hotel | /həʊˈtel/ |
| ow | how | /haʊ/ |
| ere | here | /hɪə/ |
| is | his | /hɪz/ |
| er | her | /hɜː/ |
| ave | have | /hæv/ |
| ope | hope | /həʊp/ |

**Exception!** Some words have a SILENT H:
- hour /aʊə/ (la H no se pronuncia)
- honest /ˈɒnɪst/
- heir /eə/

### Practice Sentences

1. **H**ello! **H**ow are you?
2. I **h**ope you're **h**appy.
3. **H**e **h**as a beautiful **h**ouse.
4. **H**er **h**usband is **h**ere.
5. I **h**ate **h**ot weather.

## 3. The V vs B Sound

### The Problem

In Spanish, V and B sound the same.
In English, they are DIFFERENT sounds.

- B = lips together, then open (explosión)
- V = top teeth touch bottom lip (fricción)

### The Solution

**For V:**
1. Bite your bottom lip gently with your top teeth
2. Let air pass through while making sound
3. "vvvvv" (like a vibrating sound)

**For B:**
1. Close both lips completely
2. Then release: "b!"

### Words to Practice

| B sound | V sound |
|---------|---------|
| **b**erry | **v**ery |
| **b**est | **v**est |
| **b**an | **v**an |
| **b**oat | **v**ote |
| **b**ase | **v**ase |
| cu**b** | lo**v**e |

### Minimal Pairs (practice the difference)

| B | V |
|---|---|
| berry (baya) | very (muy) |
| best (mejor) | vest (chaleco) |
| bat (murciélago) | vat (cuba) |
| ban (prohibir) | van (furgoneta) |
| bent (doblado) | vent (rejilla) |

### Practice Sentences

1. I have a **v**ery **b**ig **v**an.
2. She's **v**ery **b**eautiful.
3. I lo**v**e **b**ananas.
4. The **v**iew is **b**eautiful.
5. I **b**elieve it's **v**ery important.

## 4. The -ED Endings

### The Problem

The past tense -ED has THREE different pronunciations!
Spanish speakers often add an extra syllable: "play-ED" ❌

### The Rules

| Pronunciation | When | Examples |
|---------------|------|----------|
| **/t/** | After voiceless sounds (k, p, s, ch, sh, f) | walked, stopped, watched |
| **/d/** | After voiced sounds (b, g, v, z, m, n, l, r) | played, lived, opened |
| **/ɪd/** | After T or D sounds | wanted, needed, decided |

### The Solution

**Rule of thumb:**
- If the verb ends in T or D → add syllable: want-ED, need-ED
- Otherwise → NO extra syllable: walked (walkt), played (playd)

### Practice

| Verb | -ED form | Pronunciation | Syllables |
|------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| walk | walked | /wɔːkt/ | 1 |
| stop | stopped | /stɒpt/ | 1 |
| watch | watched | /wɒtʃt/ | 1 |
| play | played | /pleɪd/ | 1 |
| live | lived | /lɪvd/ | 1 |
| open | opened | /ˈəʊpənd/ | 2 |
| want | wanted | /ˈwɒntɪd/ | 2 |
| need | needed | /ˈniːdɪd/ | 2 |
| start | started | /ˈstɑːtɪd/ | 2 |

### Practice Sentences

1. I **walked** to school. (1 syllable: walkt)
2. She **played** tennis. (1 syllable: playd)
3. He **wanted** a coffee. (2 syllables: want-id)
4. We **watched** a movie. (1 syllable: watcht)
5. They **decided** to stay. (3 syllables: de-cid-id)

## 5. The Short I vs Long EE

### The Problem

Spanish only has one "i" sound.
English has two:
- Short I: /ɪ/ as in "ship"
- Long EE: /iː/ as in "sheep"

### The Solution

**Short I /ɪ/:**
- Relax your mouth
- Sound is shorter and lower
- Like saying "eh" but shorter

**Long EE /iː/:**
- Smile while saying it
- Sound is longer and higher
- Like Spanish "i" but longer

### Minimal Pairs

| Short I /ɪ/ | Long EE /iː/ |
|-------------|--------------|
| ship (barco) | sheep (oveja) |
| sit (sentarse) | seat (asiento) |
| hit (golpear) | heat (calor) |
| bit (trozo) | beat (vencer) |
| fill (llenar) | feel (sentir) |
| live (vivir) | leave (irse) |
| chip (patata) | cheap (barato) |

### Practice

1. The sh**i**p has sh**ee**p.
2. Please s**i**t in your s**ea**t.
3. I f**ee**l like I need to f**i**ll this form.
4. Did he l**ea**ve or does he l**i**ve here?
5. This ch**i**p is ch**ea**p.

## 6. Word Stress

### The Problem

In Spanish, stress is often on the second-to-last syllable.
In English, stress patterns are less predictable.

Wrong stress makes words hard to understand!

### Common Mistakes

| Wrong ❌ | Correct ✅ |
|----------|-----------|
| ho-TEL | HO-tel |
| mu-SIC | MU-sic |
| IN-te-res-ting | IN-ter-est-ing |
| com-FOR-ta-ble | COM-for-ta-ble |
| veg-e-TA-ble | VEG-e-ta-ble |

### Pattern: Nouns vs Verbs

Many words change stress depending on if they're nouns or verbs:

| Noun (stress on 1st) | Verb (stress on 2nd) |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| **RE**cord (disco) | re**CORD** (grabar) |
| **PRE**sent (regalo) | pre**SENT** (presentar) |
| **OB**ject (objeto) | ob**JECT** (objetar) |
| **PER**mit (permiso) | per**MIT** (permitir) |

### Practice

Say these with correct stress:

1. **PHO**-to → pho-**TO**-gra-pher → pho-to-**GRA**-phic
2. **E**-co-no-my → e-co-**NO**-mic
3. **PO**-li-tics → po-**LI**-ti-cal
4. I want to re**CORD** a **RE**cord.
5. The **PRE**sent I pre**SENT**ed was perfect.

## 7. The "TH" Sounds

### The Problem

Spanish doesn't have the English "TH" sounds.
Spanish speakers often say:
- "de" instead of "the"
- "tank" instead of "thank"
- "tree" instead of "three"

### Two TH Sounds

**Voiced TH /ð/:** the, this, that, there, they
- Tongue between teeth + vibration

**Voiceless TH /θ/:** think, three, thank, Thursday
- Tongue between teeth, NO vibration (like blowing)

### The Solution

1. Put your tongue between your teeth
2. For voiced TH: make sound (vibration)
3. For voiceless TH: blow air (no vibration)

### Practice

**Voiced TH /ð/:**
- **the**, **this**, **that**, **there**, **they**, **then**, **though**

**Voiceless TH /θ/:**
- **think**, **three**, **thank**, **Thursday**, **through**, **throw**

### Minimal Pairs

| D sound | TH sound /ð/ |
|---------|--------------|
| day | they |
| doze | those |
| dare | there |

| T sound | TH sound /θ/ |
|---------|--------------|
| tree | three |
| tank | thank |
| taught | thought |
| true | through |

### Practice Sentences

1. I **th**ink **th**ere are **th**ree people **th**ere.
2. **Th**ey **th**ink **th**is is **th**e best.
3. **Th**ank you for **th**e **th**oughtful gift.
4. **Th**ursday is **th**e **th**ird day.
5. I **th**ought **th**rough **th**e problem.

## Daily Practice Routine

### 5-Minute Daily Exercise

1. **S sounds** (1 min): Say 5 "S + consonant" words
2. **H sounds** (1 min): Say 5 words starting with H
3. **V vs B** (1 min): Practice 3 minimal pairs
4. **TH sounds** (1 min): Say "the, this, that, think, three"
5. **-ED endings** (1 min): Say 5 past tense verbs

### Tips to Improve

1. **Record yourself** - Listen and compare to native speakers
2. **Exaggerate at first** - Make the sounds bigger than necessary
3. **Slow down** - Speed comes with practice
4. **Use a mirror** - Watch your mouth position
5. **Listen and repeat** - Podcasts, songs, movies
6. **Practice tongue twisters**

### Tongue Twisters

1. **S sounds**: She sells seashells by the seashore.
2. **TH sounds**: The thirty-three thieves thought they thrilled the throne.
3. **V/B sounds**: Very brave believers behave very well.
4. **Short I/EE**: The ship with sheep shipped cheap chips.
