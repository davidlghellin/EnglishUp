# Book Reviews

## How to Write About a Book You Have Read

Learn to write about books using simple structures: plot summary, character descriptions, and opinions.

---

## Part 1: Explain the Plot (50-70 words)

Tell us what the book is about. Don't tell the ending!

### Structure
1. What type of book is it? (love story, adventure, mystery...)
2. Who are the main characters?
3. What is the main problem or situation?
4. What happens? (without the ending)

### Useful Phrases
- This book is about...
- The story takes place in... (location/time)
- The main character is...
- They have to... / They want to...
- The problem is that...

### Tense
Use **Present Simple** for plot summaries:
- The book **is** about... (NOT "was about")
- Romeo **loves** Juliet (NOT "loved")
- They **meet** in secret (NOT "met")

---

## Part 2: Describe a Character (50-80 words)

Describe one character: appearance, personality, motivations, and goals.

### Structure
1. **Introduction**: Name and basic info
2. **Physical appearance**: What do they look like?
3. **Personality**: What kind of person are they?
4. **Goals/Motivations**: What do they want?

### Useful Phrases
- [Name] is a young/old man/woman who...
- He/She comes from...
- He/She is [adjective] and [adjective]
- His/Her goal is to...
- He/She wants to...

---

## Part 3: Positive and Negative Aspects (50-80 words)

Give your opinion about the book.

### Structure
**Positive**: What did you like?
- The story / The characters / The descriptions
- What did you learn?

**Negative**: What didn't you like?
- Was it difficult to understand?
- Was it boring / too long / predictable?

### Useful Phrases
**Positive:**
- I really liked...
- The story is very interesting/exciting
- I learnt a lot about...
- My favorite part is when...

**Negative:**
- I didn't like...
- It was difficult to understand because...
- The story is too long/short
- The ending is predictable
- I had problems remembering...

## Example: Romeo and Juliet by William Shakespeare

### Part 1: Plot Summary

#### Original (with errors):
> This book is about two people, Romeo and Juliet. They have a forbidden love. They ~~came~~ from two families that hate each other and ~~were~~ always fighting in the city, but they ~~met~~ in secret.

#### Corrected version:
```
This book is about two young people, Romeo and Juliet. They have a forbidden
love. They come from two families that hate each other and are always fighting
in the city. Romeo is a Montague and Juliet is a Capulet. One night, they meet
at a party and fall in love immediately. They decide to marry in secret because
their families would never accept their relationship.
```

**Key corrections:**
| Error | Correction | Reason |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| came | come | Use Present Simple for plot summaries |
| were fighting | are fighting | Use Present Simple |
| met | meet | Use Present Simple |

---

### Part 2: Character Description

#### Original (with errors):
> Juliet is a young woman, she comes from the Capulet family. Juliet ~~is intelligent~~ girl. She is brave, ~~romantic and loves~~ Romeo. She ~~really loves Romeo to the final consequences~~.

#### Corrected version:
```
Juliet is a young woman who comes from the Capulet family. She is an intelligent
and beautiful girl, probably around 13 years old.

Juliet is brave, romantic, and very passionate. She falls in love with Romeo
even though he is from an enemy family. She is not afraid to follow her heart.

Her goal is to be with Romeo forever. She loves him so much that she is ready
to die for him.
```

**Key corrections:**
| Error | Correction | Reason |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| is intelligent girl | is **an** intelligent girl | Need article "an" before vowel |
| romantic and loves Romeo | romantic and very passionate | Avoid repetition, add variety |
| to the final consequences | ready to die for him | More natural expression |
| Comma splice | Use "who" | "Juliet is a young woman**,** she comes" → "Juliet is a young woman **who** comes" |

---

### Part 3: Positive and Negative Aspects

#### Original (with errors):
> The story is good, I never read the book before and is short with a lot of things important. ~~The true love are in the book~~, the names of the families. It's hard ~~remember~~ if Romeo is ~~from~~ Capulet or Montague.

#### Corrected version:
```
(Positive)
The story is very good. I had never read this book before, and I really enjoyed
it. It is short but has a lot of interesting scenes and beautiful descriptions.
I loved the romantic parts and the poetic language.

(Negative)
It was difficult to remember the names of all the characters. I always confused
Romeo's family (Montague) with Juliet's family (Capulet). Also, the old English
language was sometimes hard to understand.

I recommend this book because you can find a beautiful example of true love.
```

**Key corrections:**
| Error | Correction | Reason |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| I never read | I had never read | Past Perfect for "before now" |
| and is short | and it is short | Need subject "it" |
| things important | important things | Adjective before noun in English |
| The true love are | True love is | "Love" is uncountable, singular |
| It's hard remember | It was difficult to remember | Need "to" + infinitive |
| is from Capulet | is a Capulet | "Be a [family name]" not "be from" |

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Wrong tense for plot summaries
❌ Romeo loved Juliet and they got married.
✅ Romeo loves Juliet and they get married.

### 2. Missing articles
❌ She is intelligent girl.
✅ She is **an** intelligent girl.

### 3. Adjective position
❌ Something important / things interesting
✅ Something important (OK with "something/anything/nothing")
✅ **Important** things / **Interesting** scenes (adjective BEFORE noun)

### 4. Comma splice (two sentences with just a comma)
❌ Juliet is young, she comes from a rich family.
✅ Juliet is young. She comes from a rich family.
✅ Juliet is young, **and** she comes from a rich family.
✅ Juliet is a young woman **who** comes from a rich family.

### 5. Uncountable nouns
❌ The true love are beautiful.
✅ True love **is** beautiful.

## Writing Practice

### Exercise 1: Plot Summary
Write a plot summary (50-70 words) about a book or movie you know:
- Use Present Simple
- Don't reveal the ending
- Include: main characters, setting, main problem

### Exercise 2: Character Description
Choose a character and describe them (50-80 words):
- Physical appearance
- Personality (at least 3 adjectives)
- Goals or motivations

### Exercise 3: Your Opinion
Write positive and negative aspects (50-80 words):
- At least 2 positive things
- At least 1 negative thing
- Would you recommend it?

## Book Suggestions for A2 Level

Easy readers and short books to practice:
- **The Little Prince** - Antoine de Saint-Exupéry
- **Charlie and the Chocolate Factory** - Roald Dahl
- **The Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time** - Mark Haddon
- **Wonder** - R.J. Palacio
- **Graded Readers** (Oxford, Penguin, Cambridge) - Level 2/3

**Tip**: When writing about a book, make a simple plan first. Write down the main points for each section, then expand them into full sentences!
