# Making Suggestions and Negotiating

## How to Make Suggestions, Agree and Disagree in English

Learn useful phrases for conversations where you need to decide something together.

---

## Making Suggestions

Use these structures to suggest ideas:

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| **We could + verb** | We could buy him a book. |
| **How about + verb-ing?** | How about going to the cinema? |
| **How about + noun?** | How about a new laptop? |
| **Shall I / Shall we + verb?** | Shall we meet at five? |
| **Let's + verb** | Let's buy him a videogame. |
| **Why don't we + verb?** | Why don't we buy him one? |
| **Would you like to + verb?** | Would you like to come with me? |
| **What about + verb-ing/noun?** | What about pizza? |

### More Examples
- **We could** go to the beach this weekend.
- **How about** watching a movie tonight?
- **How about** that restaurant on the corner?
- **Shall we** take a taxi?
- **Let's** order some food.
- **Why don't we** invite Maria?
- **Would you like to** join us?

> **Note**: Don't repeat the same structure in one conversation. Use variety!

## Agreeing

Use these phrases to show you agree with someone:

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| **That's true.** | When someone says something correct |
| **You're right.** | When you agree with their opinion |
| **That's a good idea!** | When you like their suggestion |
| **It sounds great!** | When you're enthusiastic |
| **OK, let's do that.** | When you accept the plan |
| **Yes, I think so too.** | When you share the same opinion |
| **Sure!** | Informal agreement |
| **Good idea!** | Short, positive response |
| **Perfect!** | When you really like the idea |
| **That works for me.** | When you accept a plan/time |

---

## Disagreeing (Politely)

Use these phrases to disagree without being rude:

| Phrase | Use |
|--------|-----|
| **I don't think so.** | Soft disagreement |
| **I'm not sure about that.** | When you have doubts |
| **I think... would be better, because...** | Offer alternative + reason |
| **Yes, but...** | Partial agreement + objection |
| **That's true, but...** | Accept their point + add yours |
| **I see your point, but...** | Show understanding + disagree |
| **Actually, I think...** | Introduce different opinion |
| **Mmm... I don't know.** | Express uncertainty |

### Examples
- I don't think so. A laptop is too expensive.
- I'm not sure about that. He already has one.
- I think a book would be better, because he loves reading.
- Yes, but we don't have much money.

| Phrase | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **What do you think?** | We could buy him a watch. What do you think? |
| **Do you think...?** | Do you think he would like it? |
| **What's your opinion?** | What's your opinion about this restaurant? |
| **Is that OK with you?** | Let's meet at 5. Is that OK with you? |

## Example Dialogue: Choosing a Present

**Context**: Ana and Pablo are choosing a birthday present for their friend Hugo.

---

**Ana**: Hi, Pablo! How are you?

**Pablo**: Fine, thanks. And you?

**Ana**: Not bad, thank you. Very **busy** with my studies, but OK. By the way, did you know that Hugo's birthday is next Friday?

**Pablo**: Really?

**Ana**: Yes... He's a good friend. **We could** buy him a present. **What do you think?**

**Pablo**: Yes, **it sounds great**. What can we buy?

**Ana**: **We could** buy him a watch. **What do you think?**

**Pablo**: Mmmm... **I don't know**. You know, we don't use watches these days... many people prefer to check the time on their **smartphones**.

**Ana**: **You're right**. What shall we buy then?

**Pablo**: **How about** a new laptop? He has one, but it is very old.

**Ana**: A laptop? I think that is too **expensive**.

**Pablo**: Yes, **that's true**. I don't have much money.

**Ana**: I know he loves videogames. **Why don't we** buy him one?

**Pablo**: **That's a good idea!**

**Ana**: OK. Where can we buy it?

**Pablo**: There is a very good videogame shop on my street. **Would you like to** come to the shop and buy it **together**? Are you free tomorrow afternoon?

**Ana**: Yes. What time would you like to meet?

**Pablo**: **How about** five?

**Ana**: Five is fine. **Shall we** meet **outside** the metro station?

**Pablo**: Yes. **Let's** meet there at five. I have to go now. See you tomorrow.

**Ana**: Bye!

---

## Dialogue Structure

A negotiation dialogue usually follows this pattern:

1. **Greeting** - Hi! How are you?
2. **Introduce the topic** - Did you know that...? / I was thinking...
3. **First suggestion** - We could... / How about...?
4. **Response** (agree or disagree with reason)
5. **Alternative suggestion** (if disagreed)
6. **Agreement** - That's a good idea!
7. **Plan the details** - Where? When? What time?
8. **Confirm** - OK, let's meet at...
9. **Goodbye** - See you tomorrow!

## Common Topics for Negotiation Dialogues

### Choosing a present
- What to buy
- Where to buy it
- How much to spend

### Planning an outing
- Where to go (cinema, restaurant, park)

- How to get there

- Destination
- Transport

## Common Mistakes

❌ **How about go** to the cinema?
✅ **How about going** to the cinema?
*(How about + verb-ING)*

❌ **Let's to buy** a present.
✅ **Let's buy** a present.
*(Let's + verb WITHOUT "to")*

❌ **Shall we to meet** at five?
✅ **Shall we meet** at five?
*(Shall we + verb WITHOUT "to")*

❌ **What do you think about?**
✅ **What do you think?**
*(Don't add "about" at the end)*

---

**Tip**: In a real conversation, always give a REASON when you disagree. Just saying "no" is rude! Say "I don't think so, because..." or "That's a good idea, but..."
