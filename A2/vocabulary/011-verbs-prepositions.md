# Verbs + Prepositions

## Common Verb and Preposition Combinations

In English, many verbs are followed by specific prepositions. Learning these combinations is essential!

---

## Arrive IN / AT

### ARRIVE IN + city/country (large places)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| We **arrived in** London at 6pm | Llegamos a Londres a las 6pm |
| She **arrived in** Spain last week | Ella llegó a España la semana pasada |
| When did you **arrive in** Paris? | ¿Cuándo llegaste a París? |
| They **arrived in** Japan yesterday | Llegaron a Japón ayer |

### ARRIVE AT + specific place (buildings, stations, airports)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| We **arrived at** the airport early | Llegamos al aeropuerto temprano |
| She **arrived at** work late | Ella llegó al trabajo tarde |
| I **arrived at** the hotel at midnight | Llegué al hotel a medianoche |
| They **arrived at** the station on time | Llegaron a la estación a tiempo |

### Special: ARRIVE HOME (no preposition!)

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| I **arrived home** at 7pm | ~~I arrived at home~~ |
| She **arrived home** late | ~~She arrived to home~~ |

## Listen TO

Always use **LISTEN TO** (NOT "listen" alone):

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I **listen to** music every day | Escucho música todos los días |
| She's **listening to** a podcast | Ella está escuchando un podcast |
| Do you **listen to** the radio? | ¿Escuchas la radio? |
| We **listened to** the teacher | Escuchamos al profesor |

❌ I listen music → ✅ I **listen to** music

## Depend ON

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|

| I'm **depending on** you | Cuento contigo |

❌ It depends of the weather → ✅ It **depends on** the weather

## Look AT / Look FOR / Look AFTER

### LOOK AT = mirar (direct your eyes)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| **Look at** this photo! | ¡Mira esta foto! |
| She's **looking at** her phone | Está mirando su teléfono |
| Don't **look at** me like that | No me mires así |

### LOOK FOR = buscar (try to find)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I'm **looking for** my keys | Estoy buscando mis llaves |
| She's **looking for** a job | Está buscando trabajo |
| What are you **looking for**? | ¿Qué estás buscando? |

### LOOK AFTER = cuidar (take care of)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| Can you **look after** my cat? | ¿Puedes cuidar a mi gato? |
| She **looks after** her grandmother | Ella cuida a su abuela |
| Who's **looking after** the children? | ¿Quién cuida a los niños? |

---

## Think ABOUT / OF

### THINK ABOUT = pensar en (consider)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I'm **thinking about** my holiday | Estoy pensando en mis vacaciones |
| She's **thinking about** changing jobs | Está pensando en cambiar de trabajo |
| What are you **thinking about**? | ¿En qué estás pensando? |

### THINK OF = pensar en (have an opinion)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| What do you **think of** this idea? | ¿Qué piensas de esta idea? |
| I can't **think of** his name | No puedo recordar su nombre |
| **Think of** a number | Piensa en un número |

> Note: In many cases, ABOUT and OF are interchangeable.

---

## Talk TO / Talk ABOUT

### TALK TO = hablar con (speak with someone)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| I need to **talk to** my boss | Necesito hablar con mi jefe |
| She's **talking to** a customer | Está hablando con un cliente |
| Can I **talk to** you? | ¿Puedo hablar contigo? |

### TALK ABOUT = hablar de (discuss a topic)

| Example | Spanish |
|---------|---------|
| We **talked about** the project | Hablamos del proyecto |
| What are you **talking about**? | ¿De qué estás hablando? |
| Let's **talk about** your plans | Hablemos de tus planes |

## Pay FOR

❌ I pay the dinner → ✅ I **pay for** dinner

## Common Mistakes

| Incorrect | Correct |
|-----------|---------|
| ~~arrive to~~ London | **arrive in** London |
| ~~listen~~ music | **listen to** music |
| ~~depend of~~ | **depend on** |
| ~~wait~~ the bus | **wait for** the bus |
| ~~search~~ a job | **look for** / **search for** a job |
| ~~ask~~ help | **ask for** help |
| ~~pay~~ the bill | **pay for** the bill |
| ~~agree~~ you | **agree with** you |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Complete with the correct preposition

1. I arrived _______ Paris at midnight.
2. She's listening _______ a podcast.
3. It depends _______ the price.
4. We're waiting _______ the train.
5. I'm looking _______ my glasses. Have you seen them?
6. Can I talk _______ you for a minute?
7. Who's paying _______ lunch?
8. I agree _______ your opinion.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. in
2. to
3. on
4. for
5. for
6. to
7. for
8. with
</details>

### Exercise 2: Choose AT, IN, or FOR

1. She arrived _______ the office early.
2. We arrived _______ Italy yesterday.
3. I'm looking _______ a new apartment.
4. Look _______ that beautiful sunset!
5. I arrived _______ home late.
6. Can you ask _______ the menu?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. at
2. in
3. for
4. at
5. (no preposition) - arrived home
6. for
</details>

### Exercise 3: Correct the mistakes

1. I listen music when I work.
2. She arrived to London yesterday.
3. Can you look after at my dog?
4. I'm waiting the bus.
5. It depends of the situation.
6. Who does this belong?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. I listen **to** music when I work.
2. She arrived **in** London yesterday.
3. Can you look after my dog? (remove "at")
4. I'm waiting **for** the bus.
5. It depends **on** the situation.
6. Who does this belong **to**?
</details>

**Tip**: The preposition often doesn't match the Spanish translation! Learn verb + preposition combinations as fixed phrases, not individual words. Make flashcards with the complete phrase.
