# Paraphrasing

## Saying the Same Thing in Different Ways

Paraphrasing means expressing the same idea using different words. This is an important skill for:
- Expanding your vocabulary
- Avoiding repetition
- Showing understanding
- Exam tasks

---

## Basic Paraphrasing Techniques

### 1. Use Synonyms (words with similar meaning)

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| The house is **big** | The house is **large** |
| She is **happy** | She is **glad** / **pleased** |
| It was **difficult** | It was **hard** / **challenging** |
| The food was **delicious** | The food was **tasty** |
| I **started** work at 9 | I **began** work at 9 |
| She **finished** the project | She **completed** the project |

### 2. Change Word Forms

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| He **drives** carefully | He is a **careful driver** |
| She sings **beautifully** | She has a **beautiful voice** |
| The test was **easy** | The test wasn't **difficult** |
| He's a **fast** runner | He **runs fast** |
| She made a **decision** | She **decided** |

### 3. Use Opposite + Negative

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| The room is **small** | The room **isn't big** |
| He's **poor** | He **isn't rich** |
| She arrived **early** | She **didn't arrive late** |
| It's **cheap** | It **isn't expensive** |
| He was **unhappy** | He **wasn't happy** |

### 4. Change Active to Passive (and vice versa)

| Active | Passive |
|--------|---------|
| **Shakespeare wrote** Hamlet | Hamlet **was written by** Shakespeare |
| **They built** the house in 1990 | The house **was built** in 1990 |
| **She sent** the email | The email **was sent** |

## Paraphrasing Expressions

### Talking About Likes

| Original | Paraphrases |
|----------|-------------|
| I **like** coffee | I **enjoy** coffee |
| | I'm **fond of** coffee |
| | I'm **keen on** coffee |
| | Coffee is **my thing** |

### Talking About Frequency

| Original | Paraphrases |
|----------|-------------|
| I **always** exercise | I exercise **every day** |
| | I **never miss** a day of exercise |
| She **never** drinks coffee | She **doesn't drink** coffee **at all** |
| | She **doesn't ever** drink coffee |

### Talking About Ability

| Original | Paraphrases |
|----------|-------------|
| He **can** swim | He **is able to** swim |
| | He **knows how to** swim |
| She **can't** drive | She **isn't able to** drive |
| | She **doesn't know how to** drive |

### Talking About Necessity

| Original | Paraphrases |
|----------|-------------|
| You **must** study | You **have to** study |
| | You **need to** study |
| | It's **necessary to** study |
| You **don't have to** pay | You **don't need to** pay |
| | It's **not necessary to** pay |

### Talking About Possibility

| Original | Paraphrases |
|----------|-------------|
| **Maybe** he's at home | He **might be** at home |
| | He's **probably** at home |
| | He **could be** at home |

## Paraphrasing Sentences

### Example 1: Daily Routine

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| I wake up at 7am | I get up at 7 in the morning |
| I have breakfast at 8 | I eat breakfast at 8 o'clock |
| I go to work by bus | I take the bus to work |
| I get home at 6pm | I arrive home at 6 in the evening |

### Example 2: Describing People

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| She has long hair | Her hair is long |
| He is tall | He's a tall man |
| She's friendly | She's a friendly person |
| He's very intelligent | He's really smart |

### Example 3: Weather

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| It's raining | It's wet outside |
| | The weather is rainy |
| It's very hot | The temperature is high |
| | It's boiling |
| It's cold | The weather is cold |
| | It's freezing |

### Example 4: Opinions

| Original | Paraphrase |
|----------|------------|
| I think it's good | In my opinion, it's good |
| | I believe it's good |
| | It seems good to me |
| I don't like it | I'm not keen on it |
| | It's not my thing |

## Useful Paraphrasing Phrases

### To Express the Same Idea

- In other words...
- That is to say...
- What I mean is...
- To put it another way...

### To Give an Example

- For example...
- For instance...
- Such as...
- Like...

### To Summarize

- In short...
- To sum up...
- Basically...
- In brief...

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Find the synonym

Match the word with its synonym:

1. big → ___
2. happy → ___
3. difficult → ___
4. start → ___
5. fast → ___

Options: a) begin, b) large, c) quick, d) hard, e) glad

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. b) large
2. e) glad
3. d) hard
4. a) begin
5. c) quick
</details>

### Exercise 2: Paraphrase the sentences

Rewrite these sentences using different words:

1. The movie was very good.
   → The movie was _______________

2. She can speak three languages.
   → She is _______________ three languages.

3. I always go to the gym.
   → I go to the gym _______________

4. The test was easy.
   → The test wasn't _______________

5. You must wear a seatbelt.
   → You _______________ wear a seatbelt.

<details>
<summary>Possible Answers</summary>

1. The movie was excellent/great/wonderful.
2. She is able to speak three languages.
3. I go to the gym every day.
4. The test wasn't difficult.
5. You have to / need to wear a seatbelt.
</details>

### Exercise 3: Paraphrase the paragraph

Original:
> I like going to the beach. The weather is usually hot and I can swim in the sea. I think it's very relaxing.

Write the same idea using different words:

<details>
<summary>Possible Answer</summary>

I enjoy visiting the beach. The temperature is normally high and I'm able to swim in the ocean. In my opinion, it's really calming.
</details>

## Exam Tip

In speaking and writing exams, paraphrasing shows:
- You understand the question
- You have a wide vocabulary
- You can express ideas in different ways

**Don't just repeat the question!**

❌ "Do you like sports?" → "Yes, I like sports."
✅ "Do you like sports?" → "Yes, I'm really keen on sports, especially football."

---

**Tip**: When you learn a new word, always learn 2-3 synonyms too. This will help you paraphrase naturally and expand your vocabulary.
