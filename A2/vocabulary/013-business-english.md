## Meeting Phrases

### Pausing a Topic

When you need to stop discussing something and return to it later:

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **Let's put a pin on it** | Vamos a dejarlo aquí y volver a ello | "Let's **put a pin on it** and come back to this later." |
| **Let's circle back** | Volvamos a esto después | "Let's **circle back** to this tomorrow." |
| **Hold that thought** | Espera con esa idea | "**Hold that thought** for a second." |
| **Let's table this** | Dejémoslo para después | "Let's **table this** for now." |
| **We'll revisit this** | Volveremos a esto | "**We'll revisit this** in the next meeting." |

### Common vs Professional

| Basic (correct but less natural) | Professional (more natural) |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Let's talk about this again later | **Let's circle back** to this |
| Wait a moment | **Hold that thought** |
| We'll discuss this later | **Let's put a pin on it** |

## Talking About Work

### Side Jobs and Extra Work

| Expression | Meaning | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| **side hustle** | trabajo secundario | "I have a **side hustle** doing consulting." |
| **on the side** | como extra, aparte | "I do freelance work **on the side**." |
| **moonlighting** | pluriempleo | "He's **moonlighting** as a tutor." |

**Examples:**
- I have a **side hustle** selling things online.
- She does photography **on the side**.
- My main job is programming, but I do consulting **on the side**.

### Workload

| Basic | Professional |
|-------|-------------|
| I have a lot of work | I have a **heavy workload** |
| I'm very busy | I'm **swamped** / I'm **snowed under** |
| Not much work | A **light workload** |

**Examples:**
- I have a **heavy workload** this week, so I can't meet.
- Things are quiet - I have a **light workload** at the moment.
- I'm completely **swamped** with deadlines.

## Cause and Effect Connectors

### The Problem with "Because"

Spanish speakers often use "because" alone, leaving the idea incomplete:

❌ "Companies don't trust freelancers. Because they prefer big firms."

✅ "Companies don't trust freelancers, **that's why** they hire big firms."

### Better Connectors

| Connector | Use | Example |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| **that's why** | resultado informal | "The deadline is tomorrow, **that's why** I'm stressed." |
| **so** | resultado | "I was tired, **so** I went to bed early." |
| **as a result** | resultado formal | "Sales dropped; **as a result**, we cut costs." |
| **therefore** | resultado muy formal | "The data is incomplete. **Therefore**, we can't proceed." |
| **consequently** | resultado formal | "He missed the deadline. **Consequently**, he lost the client." |

### Examples

| Basic ❌ | Natural ✅ |
|----------|-----------|
| Big companies have more resources. Because freelancers have less. | Big companies have more resources, **that's why** freelancers struggle. |
| I didn't sleep well. Because I drank coffee late. | I drank coffee late, **so** I didn't sleep well. |
| The project failed. Because we didn't plan. | We didn't plan properly; **as a result**, the project failed. |

## Explaining Technical Work to Non-Technical People

### Using Analogies

When explaining complex concepts, use comparisons:

| Technical | Simple Analogy |
|-----------|----------------|
| I work with databases | "I organize information, **like a digital librarian**." |
| I do data analysis | "I find patterns in numbers, **like a detective**." |
| I'm a consultant | "I help companies solve problems, **like a doctor for businesses**." |
| I work in cybersecurity | "I protect computers from hackers, **like a security guard**." |

### Useful Phrases

- "**Think of it like...**" + simple comparison
- "**It's similar to...**" + everyday example
- "**Basically, what I do is...**" + simple explanation
- "**In simple terms...**" + easy version
- "**Imagine...**" + relatable scenario

### Example

❌ "I work with ETL pipelines and data warehousing for business intelligence."

✅ "**Basically**, I help companies understand their data. **Think of it like** organizing a messy room - I take all the information and put it in order so people can find what they need."

## Handling Fast Native Speakers

### Asking for Clarification (Professionally)

Don't apologize too much! Be confident:

| Too apologetic ❌ | Confident ✅ |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Sorry, sorry, I don't understand | **Could you slow down a bit?** |
| Sorry, my English is bad | **Could you repeat that?** |
| I'm sorry, what? | **What do you mean by...?** |

### Useful Phrases

- "**Could you repeat that**, please?"
- "**Could you speak a bit slower?**"
- "**What do you mean by** [word]?"
- "**Just to clarify**, are you saying...?"
- "**Let me make sure I understand**..."
- "**So, if I understand correctly**..."

## Email Phrases

### Starting

| Informal | Formal |
|----------|--------|
| Hi [name] | Dear [name] |
| Hope you're well | I hope this email finds you well |
| Quick question... | I am writing to inquire about... |

### Ending

| Informal | Formal |
|----------|--------|
| Thanks! | Thank you for your time |
| Let me know | Please let me know if you need anything |
| Talk soon | I look forward to hearing from you |
| Best | Best regards / Kind regards |

### Useful Phrases

- "**As discussed**..." (como hablamos)
- "**Please find attached**..." (adjunto...)
- "**Just following up on**..." (siguiendo con...)
- "**I wanted to check in about**..." (quería preguntar sobre...)
- "**Apologies for the delay**..." (perdón por el retraso)

## Common Collocations

Learn these as complete phrases:

| Collocation | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| **heavy** workload | I have a heavy workload |
| **tight** deadline | We have a tight deadline |
| **attend** a meeting | I need to attend a meeting |
| **run** a meeting | She's running the meeting |
| **meet** a deadline | Did you meet the deadline? |
| **miss** a deadline | I missed the deadline |
| **make** a decision | We need to make a decision |
| **reach** an agreement | We reached an agreement |
| **raise** an issue | I'd like to raise an issue |
| **address** a problem | We need to address this problem |

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Make it professional

Rewrite these sentences using professional expressions:

1. Let's talk about this again tomorrow.
2. I have a lot of work this week.
3. Wait, I want to say something.
4. I do extra work as a freelancer.
5. The client was unhappy. Because we were late.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Let's **circle back** to this tomorrow.
2. I have a **heavy workload** this week.
3. **Hold that thought**.
4. I do freelance work **on the side**. / I have a **side hustle**.
5. We were late, **that's why** the client was unhappy. / We were late; **as a result**, the client was unhappy.
</details>

### Exercise 2: Explain simply

Explain these jobs to a 10-year-old:

1. Software developer
2. Data analyst
3. Project manager
4. Marketing specialist

<details>
<summary>Possible Answers</summary>

1. "I tell computers what to do. **Think of it like** writing instructions for a robot."
2. "I look at numbers to find interesting things. **It's like** being a detective, but with data."
3. "I make sure everyone works together to finish things on time. **It's similar to** a football coach."
4. "I help people learn about products they might like. **Think of it like** making posters for a school play."
</details>

## Pronunciation Tips

### Stress in Collocations

Practice stressing the right syllable:

- **HEA**vy **WORK**load (not heaVY workLOAD)
- **SIDE** hustle (not side HUStle)
- **CIR**cle back (not cirCLE BACK)
- **DEAD**line (not deadLINE)

### Intonation When Explaining

- **Slow down** when introducing new ideas
- **Pause** between steps or concepts
- **Raise pitch** slightly on key words
- **Lower pitch** at the end of explanations

---

**Tip**: In professional English, sounding natural is more important than being grammatically perfect. Learn complete phrases, not just individual words!
