# Emails and Messages

## Writing Informal Emails and Messages

Learn to write simple emails to friends and basic formal emails.

## Informal Emails

### Structure
1. **Greeting**: Hi / Hello / Dear [name]
2. **Opening**: How are you? / Hope you're well
3. **Main message**: Your news or request
4. **Closing**: See you soon / Take care / Best wishes
5. **Sign off**: Your name

### Example - Email to a Friend

```
Hi Sarah,

How are you? I hope you're doing well.

I wanted to tell you about my new job. I started working at a bookstore
last week and I really like it. The people are very friendly and the
hours are good.

Do you want to meet for coffee this weekend? We can catch up!

Let me know when you're free.

Take care,
Maria
```

## Formal Emails

### Structure
1. **Greeting**: Dear Sir/Madam / Dear [name]
2. **Purpose**: I am writing to...
3. **Main message**: Clear and polite
4. **Closing**: Thank you / I look forward to...
5. **Sign off**: Yours sincerely / Best regards

### Example - Booking Confirmation

```
Dear Mr. Johnson,

I am writing to confirm my booking at your hotel.

I would like to book a double room for two nights, from July 15th to
July 17th. Could you please send me the confirmation and the price?

Thank you for your help.

Best regards,
Maria Lopez
```

## Text Messages

Keep them short and clear:
- "Can we meet at 5pm?"
- "Thanks for the invitation!"
- "Sorry, I'm running late"
- "See you tomorrow"

## "Looking Forward To" + verb-ING

This is a very common phrase for ending emails. It means you are excited or waiting for something.

### Structure
**Look forward to + verb-ING** (NOT infinitive!)

> **Why -ING?** Because "to" here is a PREPOSITION, not part of an infinitive. After prepositions, we always use -ING.

### Examples

| Correct | Incorrect |
|---------|-----------|
| I'm looking forward to **seeing** you | ~~looking forward to see~~ |
| I look forward to **hearing** from you | ~~look forward to hear~~ |
| We're looking forward to **meeting** you | ~~looking forward to meet~~ |
| I'm looking forward to **starting** my new job | ~~looking forward to start~~ |
| She's looking forward to **going** on holiday | ~~looking forward to go~~ |

### Formal vs Informal

| Formal (emails, letters) | Informal (friends) |
|--------------------------|-------------------|
| I look forward to hearing from you | I'm looking forward to seeing you! |
| I look forward to your reply | Looking forward to the party! |
| I look forward to meeting you | Can't wait to see you! |

### Common Uses in Emails

**Ending formal emails:**
- I look forward to hearing from you.
- I look forward to your reply.
- I look forward to meeting you soon.
- I look forward to receiving your confirmation.

**Ending informal emails:**
- Looking forward to seeing you!
- Looking forward to the weekend!
- I'm really looking forward to your visit!

### Similar Structures (Preposition + -ING)

Other expressions that use "to" as a preposition (NOT infinitive):

| Expression | Example |
|------------|---------|
| be used to + -ing | I'm used to **working** at night |
| get used to + -ing | I'm getting used to **living** here |
| object to + -ing | I object to **paying** extra |
| admit to + -ing | He admitted to **stealing** the money |
| be committed to + -ing | We're committed to **helping** you |

### Common Mistakes

❌ I look forward to **see** you → ✅ I look forward to **seeing** you
❌ I'm looking forward to **meet** you → ✅ I'm looking forward to **meeting** you
❌ Looking forward to **hear** from you → ✅ Looking forward to **hearing** from you

## Pen Pal Letters (Cartas de presentación)

Writing to someone you don't know well. Introduce yourself and share personal information.

### Structure
1. **Greeting**: Hello / Hi [name]
2. **Thank them**: Thank you for your email/letter
3. **Introduction**: Name, age, where you live, job
4. **Family**: Brothers, sisters, parents
5. **Hobbies and interests**: What you like to do
6. **Personality**: What kind of person you are
7. **Closing**: I have to go now because... / Looking forward to hearing from you
8. **Sign off**: Best regards / Take care
9. **PS** (optional): Extra information

### Useful Phrases

**Introduction:**
- My name is... and I am... years old
- I was born in... (city/country)
- I live in... / I'm from...
- I work as a... / I am a... (job)
- I started working at... in... (month/year)

**Family:**
- I have a sister/brother called...
- My family is... (big/small)
- I live with my... / I live alone

**Physical description:**
- I am tall/short/medium height
- I have... hair (long/short, brown/blonde)
- I am bald (no hair)
- I wear glasses

**Hobbies:**
- I like + verb-ing (I like reading)
- I enjoy + verb-ing (I enjoy cooking)
- I'm interested in + noun (I'm interested in music)
- I spend a lot of time + verb-ing

**Personality:**
- I am friendly/shy/outgoing
- I consider myself... (adjective)
- Maybe I'm not very good at... but I'm trying to improve

**Likes:**
- I like... / I love... / I enjoy...
- My favorite... is...
- I'm a fan of...

### Key Corrections

| Error | Correction | Type |
|-------|-----------|------|
| I born in | I **was born** in | Past passive |
| like my sister | like my sister (OK) / **the same as** my sister | Clarification |
| I started 1 October **in** new company | I started **on** 1st October **in a** new company | Preposition + article |
| I am Senior consultant | I am **a** Senior Consultant | Article |
| The family you know Ana | Ana is my sister | Rewrite for clarity |
| she is very pretty | OK (correct) | - |
| my fathers | my **parents** | parents = father + mother |
| I go to the gym all days | I go to the gym **every day** | "all days" doesn't exist |
| I have not hair | I have **no** hair / I **don't have** hair | Negation |
| I am not lose my time | I **don't waste** my time | Verb form |
| I am not lose my time in the travel | I don't waste any time **travelling to work** | Natural expression |
| I think is bad day | I think **it is a** bad day | Subject + article |
| on other hand | **on the other** hand | Article |
| my customer see | my customer **will see** | Future tense |
| wants that I change | will want **to hire me** | Infinitive structure |
| I am comitter | I am **a committer** | Article |
| that in the near future is | that in the near future **will be** | Future tense |
| How it catch | How it **catches** | 3rd person -s |
| I don't like the people | I don't like people | No article with general nouns |
| I need to learn talk | I need to learn **to talk** | to + infinitive |
| I was a freelance | I was a **freelancer** | Noun form |
| I need to search new customers | I need to search **for** new customers | Phrasal verb |
| I like learn all days something | I like **learning new things every day** | Word order + gerund |
| T-Bone steacks | T-Bone **steaks** | Spelling |
| I need go to rest | I need **to go** to rest | to + infinitive |

### Common Mistakes in Pen Pal Letters

**1. Missing articles (a/an/the)**
- ❌ I am student → ✅ I am **a** student
- ❌ I work as teacher → ✅ I work as **a** teacher
- ❌ I am Senior Consultant → ✅ I am **a** Senior Consultant

**2. "All days" vs "Every day"**
- ❌ I go to the gym all days → ✅ I go to the gym **every day**
- ❌ I work all days → ✅ I work **every day**

**3. "I was born" (not "I born")**
- ❌ I born in Madrid → ✅ I **was born** in Madrid

**4. General nouns (no article)**
- ❌ I don't like the people → ✅ I don't like **people**
- ❌ I like the music → ✅ I like **music**
- ❌ The life is good → ✅ **Life** is good

**5. "Need to" + infinitive**
- ❌ I need go → ✅ I need **to go**
- ❌ I need learn → ✅ I need **to learn**

**6. "Learn to" + infinitive**
- ❌ I need to learn talk → ✅ I need to learn **to talk**

**7. Search FOR something**
- ❌ I search new customers → ✅ I search **for** new customers

## Writing Practice

### Exercise 1: Write an informal email
Write an email to a friend about:
- Your last weekend
- An invitation to do something together

### Exercise 2: Write a formal email
Write an email to a hotel to:
- Ask about room availability
- Ask about prices
- Request information

### Exercise 3: Write a pen pal letter
Write a letter introducing yourself (150-200 words). Include:
- Your name, age, and where you live
- Your job or studies
- Your family
- Your hobbies (at least 3)
- Your personality
- Something interesting about you
- A question for your pen pal

---

**Tip**: In informal emails you can use contractions (I'm, don't, can't). In formal emails, write the full forms (I am, do not, cannot).
