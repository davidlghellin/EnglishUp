# Emails and Messages

## Writing Informal Emails and Messages

Learn to write simple emails to friends and basic formal emails.

## Informal Emails

### Structure
1. **Greeting**: Hi / Hello / Dear [name]
2. **Opening**: How are you? / Hope you're well
3. **Main message**: Your news or request
4. **Closing**: See you soon / Take care / Best wishes
5. **Sign off**: Your name

### Example - Email to a Friend

```
Hi Sarah,

How are you? I hope you're doing well.

I wanted to tell you about my new job. I started working at a bookstore
last week and I really like it. The people are very friendly and the
hours are good.

Do you want to meet for coffee this weekend? We can catch up!

Let me know when you're free.

Take care,
Maria
```

## Formal Emails

### Structure
1. **Greeting**: Dear Sir/Madam / Dear [name]
2. **Purpose**: I am writing to...
3. **Main message**: Clear and polite
4. **Closing**: Thank you / I look forward to...
5. **Sign off**: Yours sincerely / Best regards

### Example - Booking Confirmation

```
Dear Mr. Johnson,

I am writing to confirm my booking at your hotel.

I would like to book a double room for two nights, from July 15th to
July 17th. Could you please send me the confirmation and the price?

Thank you for your help.

Best regards,
Maria Lopez
```

## Text Messages

Keep them short and clear:
- "Can we meet at 5pm?"
- "Thanks for the invitation!"
- "Sorry, I'm running late"
- "See you tomorrow"

## Writing Practice

### Exercise 1: Write an informal email
Write an email to a friend about:
- Your last weekend
- An invitation to do something together

### Exercise 2: Write a formal email
Write an email to a hotel to:
- Ask about room availability
- Ask about prices
- Request information

---

**Tip**: In informal emails you can use contractions (I'm, don't, can't). In formal emails, write the full forms (I am, do not, cannot).
