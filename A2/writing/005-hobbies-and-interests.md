# Hobbies and Interests

## Writing About Your Hobbies

Learn to write articles about hobbies like drawing, cooking, sports, and other activities you enjoy.

## Key Vocabulary

### Creative Hobbies
- **Drawing/Painting**: sketch, colors, canvas, brush, pencil, portrait, landscape
- **Cooking/Baking**: recipe, ingredients, dish, oven, stir, mix, fry, bake
- **Photography**: camera, photo, shot, focus, light, capture
- **Music**: play, instrument, guitar, piano, practice, song, melody
- **Crafts**: knitting, sewing, pottery, create, handmade

### Sports and Physical Activities
- **Individual**: running, swimming, cycling, yoga, gym, workout
- **Team sports**: football, basketball, volleyball, tennis
- **Outdoor**: hiking, climbing, camping, fishing

### Other Hobbies
- **Gaming**: video games, board games, puzzle, strategy
- **Reading**: book, novel, magazine, chapter, story
- **Gardening**: plants, flowers, seeds, water, grow
- **Open Source/Programming**: code, contribute, collaborate, review, documentation, framework, project, repository, community

## Useful Phrases

### Talking About Frequency
- I **always/usually/often/sometimes/rarely/never** + verb
- I do this **every day/week/weekend/month**
- I practice **twice a week / three times a month**

### Expressing Likes and Preferences
- I **love/enjoy/like** + verb-ing
- My favorite hobby is...
- I'm **passionate about / interested in** + noun/verb-ing
- I **prefer** doing X **to** doing Y

### Explaining Why You Like It
- It **helps me relax / makes me happy**
- It's a great way to **spend time / express myself**
- I find it **relaxing / exciting / creative**
- It's **good for** my health / my mind

## Structure for a Hobby Article

1. **Introduction**: What is the hobby? When did you start?
2. **Description**: What do you do? What do you need?
3. **Why you like it**: How does it make you feel?
4. **Conclusion**: Would you recommend it?

## Example 1: Drawing

```
My hobby is drawing. I started drawing when I was a child, and I still love
it today.

I usually draw portraits of people or landscapes. I use pencils and sometimes
colored pencils. I don't need many materials - just a sketchbook and a pencil
are enough to start.

I draw for about two hours every weekend. Sometimes I draw at home, but when
the weather is nice, I like to go to the park and draw what I see around me.

I love drawing because it helps me relax after a busy week. When I draw, I
forget about my problems and I feel calm. It's also a great way to express
my creativity.

If you want to try a new hobby, I recommend drawing. You don't need expensive
materials and you can do it anywhere!
```

## Example 2: Cooking

```
One of my favorite hobbies is cooking. I started learning to cook five years
ago, and now I cook almost every day.

I love making Italian food, especially pasta and pizza. Every Sunday, I try
a new recipe from the internet or from a cookbook. I enjoy going to the market
to buy fresh ingredients like vegetables, meat, and herbs.

Cooking is a creative activity for me. I like experimenting with different
flavors and ingredients. Sometimes my dishes are delicious, and sometimes they
are not very good - but I always learn something new!

I enjoy cooking because it's relaxing and I can share my food with family and
friends. There's nothing better than seeing people happy when they eat something
I made.

If you're interested in cooking, start with simple recipes and don't be afraid
to make mistakes. Practice makes perfect!
```

## Example 3: Running

```
My hobby is running. I started running three years ago because I wanted to
be healthier.

I run three times a week - on Monday, Wednesday, and Saturday. I usually run
in the park near my house for about 30 minutes. I wake up early in the morning
when the air is fresh and there are not many people.

I need comfortable running shoes, shorts, and a t-shirt. When it's cold, I
wear a jacket. I also use a phone app to track my distance and time.

Running helps me feel energetic and positive. After a run, I feel stronger
and happier. It's also a good way to clear my mind and think about my life.

Last year, I ran my first 10K race. It was hard, but I felt very proud when
I finished. Now my goal is to run a half marathon.

I recommend running to everyone. It's free, simple, and very good for your
body and mind!
```

## Example 4: Playing Guitar

```
Playing guitar is my favorite hobby. I started learning when I was 15 years
old, and now I can't imagine my life without music.

I have an acoustic guitar that I bought with my first salary. I practice for
about one hour every day after work. I learn songs from YouTube tutorials and
sometimes I create my own music.

I mostly play pop and rock songs, but I also like classical guitar music.
My favorite song to play is "Wonderwall" by Oasis - it was the first song I
learned completely.

I love playing guitar because it's a wonderful way to express my emotions.
When I'm sad, I play slow songs. When I'm happy, I play something fast and fun.
Music always makes me feel better.

Sometimes I play guitar with my friends. We meet at someone's house and play
together. It's a great way to spend time with people I like.

If you want to learn an instrument, start with guitar. It's not too difficult
and you can learn many songs quickly!
```

## Example 5: Contributing to Open Source Projects

```
My hobby is helping and collaborating in open source projects. It's writing
code, documentation, and reviewing code with people around the world.

I started 6 months ago because I was a freelancer and I needed to look for
new customers. For this reason, I started to talk about being a Senior
Committer in a framework called 'Apache Spark'.

I like it because I learn, earn money, and meet many people around the world.
Also, very importantly, I like my job. With this hobby, I can apply these
tools to my job. These open source projects maybe will be used by some
companies and, who knows, Google, AWS or Tesla might hire me for new roles.

At the moment, some companies hire me for my hobby and this is great. My
hobby pays me some invoices (hehe).

This hobby is not for everyone because you can't disconnect your job from
your hobby, and sometimes the schedule is strange. But I recommend this for
people in IT jobs.
```

## Writing Practice

### Exercise 1: Your Favorite Hobby
Write about your main hobby (8-10 sentences):
- What is your hobby?
- When and why did you start?
- How often do you do it?
- What do you need for this hobby?
- Why do you enjoy it?

### Exercise 2: A Creative Hobby
Write about a creative activity like cooking, drawing, photography, or music (8-10 sentences):
- Describe the activity
- Explain the process
- Say why it's satisfying
- Give advice for beginners

### Exercise 3: A Physical Hobby
Write about a sport or physical activity you do (8-10 sentences):
- What sport/activity is it?
- How often do you practice?
- What equipment do you need?
- What are the benefits?
- Would you recommend it? Why?

### Exercise 4: A Hobby You Want to Try
Write about a hobby you would like to start (6-8 sentences):
- What hobby do you want to try?
- Why does it interest you?
- What would you need to start?
- What difficulties might you have?

---

**Tip**: When writing about hobbies, use specific details and personal examples. This makes your writing more interesting and authentic!
