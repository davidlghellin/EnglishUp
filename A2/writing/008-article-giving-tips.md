# Article Giving Tips ("How to survive...")

## Writing Task (A2)

Write a **"How to survive..."** article giving tips/recommendations.

**Topics:**
- How to survive a **holiday with your family**
- How to survive a **job interview** ✅
- How to survive a **party where you don't know anyone**

---

## Structure (4 paragraphs)

```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  PARAGRAPH 1 → Introduction                    │
│     General idea about the situation           │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PARAGRAPH 2 → Problem 1 + Tips                │
│     Topic sentence (problem) + imperatives     │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PARAGRAPH 3 → Problem 2 + Tips                │
│     Topic sentence (problem) + imperatives     │
├────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  PARAGRAPH 4 → Problem 3 + Tips                │
│     Topic sentence (problem) + imperatives     │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Topic Sentences

> *A paragraph is about one topic, and we introduce the topic in the first sentence.*
> *When we give advice, we use the topic sentence to **describe the problem**, and then give tips with **positive or negative imperatives**.*

**Example:**
- Topic sentence (problem): *People have different ideas about how much money they want to spend.*
- Tips (imperatives): *Don't wait until you arrive and have an argument. Discuss what you expect to pay for...*

---

## Imperatives (the key grammar!)

### Positive imperative (do this!)
- **Plan** to arrive 10 minutes early.
- **Smile** when you say hello.
- **Listen** carefully to the questions.

### Negative imperative (don't do this!)
- **Don't be** late.
- **Don't speak** too fast.
- **Don't forget** to research the company.

### Soft advice (less direct)
- **Try to** stay calm.
- **Make sure (that)** you sleep well the night before.
- **It's a good idea to** prepare some questions.
- **Remember to** turn off your phone.

---

## Useful Verbs for Imperatives (Job Interview)

| Positive ✅ | Negative ❌ |
|------------|------------|
| **Prepare** answers to common questions | **Don't arrive** late |
| **Research** the company before the interview | **Don't wear** casual clothes |
| **Dress** smartly | **Don't interrupt** the interviewer |
| **Smile** and shake hands | **Don't lie** about your experience |
| **Listen** carefully | **Don't speak** too fast |
| **Ask** questions at the end | **Don't forget** to turn off your phone |
| **Make** eye contact | **Don't say** anything bad about old jobs |

## Sample Article (100-120 words) ✍️

### **How to survive... a remote IT job interview**

Remote IT interviews can be stressful, but with good preparation you can feel confident and do well.

In remote interviews, there are often technical problems with the connection. Test your camera, microphone and internet before you start, and make sure you can share your screen.

Many candidates lie about their skills, but companies notice on the first day. Be honest. If you use AI tools at work, say so, but explain that you guide the prompts and review the code.

In technical interviews, it's easy to give only basic answers. When they ask you something, talk about how to optimize the solution. Don't speak badly about your old jobs and practice often — interviews in English are free classes!

*(117 words)*

## Top Tips for This Writing Task

### 1. Each paragraph = ONE topic
- Paragraph 2 → ONE problem (e.g. preparation)
- Paragraph 3 → ANOTHER problem (e.g. arriving / appearance)
- Paragraph 4 → ANOTHER problem (e.g. during the interview)

❌ Don't mix all the tips in one paragraph.
✅ Group tips by topic.

### 2. Always start with a topic sentence
The first sentence describes the **problem** or **situation**. The other sentences give the **tips**.

### 3. Mix positive AND negative imperatives
Don't use only "do" or only "don't". Combine them:
- *Dress smartly. **Don't wear** casual clothes.*
- *Smile and shake hands. **Don't forget** to turn off your phone.*

### 4. Use linking words (A2 level)
- **Also** / **And** → adding tips
- **But** → contrast
- **Because** → giving a reason
- **So** → result
- **For example** → giving an example

### 5. Common A2 Mistakes

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Correct |
|----------|-----------|
| You **must to** prepare | You **must prepare** / **Prepare** |
| **No** speak too fast | **Don't** speak too fast |
| Is important to arrive early | **It is** important to arrive early |
| **For** be on time, plan ahead | **To be** on time, plan ahead |
| Make a good impression **for** smile | Make a good impression **by** smiling |
| **Remember turn off** your phone | **Remember to turn off** your phone |

### 6. Final Checklist

- [ ] **Title** with "How to survive..."
- [ ] **4 paragraphs** (intro + 3 problems)
- [ ] Each paragraph starts with a **topic sentence** (the problem)
- [ ] I used **positive AND negative imperatives**
- [ ] **100-120 words**
- [ ] I checked spelling, punctuation, and grammar
